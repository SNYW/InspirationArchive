# The Incredible Melting Man (1977)

**Tags:** 🌍 USA | 🎭 Gritty, Tense | 📚 Horror | 🧬 Evolution, Isolation, Technology

**Medium:** Film

**Creator/Author:** William Sachs (Director/Writer)

## The Pitch

An astronaut returns from a mission to Saturn with a devastating secret—radiation exposure has begun to melt his flesh away, driving him to murder and cannibalism. As the creature that was once Colonel Steve West escapes from the hospital, a scientist who was his friend and a general must track him down before he claims more victims. Meanwhile, the scientist believes West may be getting stronger the more his body decomposes.

## The Truth

During a space flight to Saturn, three astronauts are exposed to a blast of radiation which kills two of them and seriously injures the third, Colonel Steve West. Back on Earth, West awakens in a hospital to find the flesh on his face and hands melting away. Hysterical, he attacks and kills a nurse, then escapes.

Dr. Theodore "Ted" Nelson, a scientist and friend of West, discovers that the nurse's corpse is emitting feeble radiation and realizes West's body has become radioactive. Nelson believes West has gone insane and must consume human flesh to slow the melting. He calls General Michael Perry, who agrees to help find West.

West attacks and kills a fisherman in the woods, then encounters a little girl named Carol, who escapes unharmed. Later, Nelson and Perry arrive at the crime scene. Sheriff Neil Blake becomes suspicious of Nelson but is told nothing due to classification.

That night, West attacks and kills an elderly couple. When their bodies are found, Nelson reluctantly reveals West's condition, believing West is somehow getting stronger the more his body decomposes. West then attacks Nelson's home—General Perry is killed but Judy (Nelson's pregnant wife) escapes.

Nelson and Blake track West to a giant power plant. Blake tries to shoot West but is killed. Nelson is left hanging from a railing and appeals to West, reminding him of their friendship. West decides to pull Nelson to safety, but armed security guards arrive and panic, fatally shooting Nelson in the face as he tries to protect West. Enraged, West kills the guards and stumbles away.

West collapses against a building and with no hope of survival, slowly and completely melts away. The next morning, a janitor finds his gory remains and casually mops them into a garbage can. Enthusiastic radio reports announce the next crewed mission to Saturn.

## The Core

The Incredible Melting Man represents a fascinating artifact of 1970s body horror, emerging from the same atomic-age anxiety that produced films like The Thing and Shivers. The film serves as a dark meditation on humanity's relationship with technology and the cosmos—Steve West's transformation into a monster is a direct consequence of humanity's reach toward Saturn, suggesting that space exploration carries inherent horrors we cannot anticipate or control.

Director William Sachs originally intended this as a parody of horror films, but producers retooled it into a serious body horror piece. The final film carries tonal inconsistencies—a darkly comic ending where a janitor casually mops up the monster's remains alongside genuinely disturbing makeup effects from a pre-fame Rick Baker. This tension between satire and genuine horror gives the film an unusual quality.

The film also explores themes of isolation and loss of identity. West becomes increasingly monstrous yet retains fragments of his humanity—he spares the child Carol and ultimately saves Nelson, suggesting the man inside the melting monster still exists. The ending, which announces another mission to Saturn with cheerful disregard for the previous disaster, critiques humanity's tendency to ignore the warnings embedded in catastrophic events.
