## The Pitch

Welcome to the Space Show is a 2010 Japanese animated science fiction film produced by A-1 Pictures and distributed by Aniplex. Directed by Koji Masunari from a script by Hideyuki Kurata, the film tells the story of five elementary school children who rescue an alien dog and are rewarded with a trip to the Moon. When circumstances leave them stranded in space, they must journey across the galaxy to return home before their parents arrive for the school trip. The film was described by its producers as surpassing Star Wars in scale, and premiered at the 60th Berlin International Film Festival in February 2010.

## The Truth

During summer vacation, five elementary school classmates—Natsuki, Amane, Kiyoshi, Noriko, and Kōji—are looking for their missing class pet rabbit, Pyon-Kichi. In a corn field, they discover an injured dog who turns out to be an alien named Pochi Rickman from the planet Wan. As gratitude for helping him, Pochi invites the children to visit the Moon.

However, after a series of strange events, the children become stranded in space and must travel across the galaxy to reach Pochi's homeworld Wan. Along the way, they discover they are being pursued by aliens working for Neppo, the host of "The Space Show"—the universe's most popular entertainment broadcast from a mysterious spaceship called the Pet Star.

Neppo seeks a substance called Zughan to power his ship, which turns out to be related to wasabi and functions as a recreational drug. Since Natsuki unknowingly carries some in her bag, Neppo pursues the children relentlessly. When they finally reach Wan, Neppo's tractor beams kidnap both their supplies and Amane.

The children must face Neppo at an interstellar festival, where with help from friends they've made along the journey—including alien mechanic Ink and her father—they manage to defeat Neppo, rescue Amane, and save the entire universe. They return home with Pyon-Kichi just as their parents arrive for the school trip.

## The Core

**Friendship and Childhood Adventure:** At its core, the film celebrates the power of childhood friendship and imagination. The five children—despite their differences and occasional quarrels—work together to overcome enormous challenges. Their journey represents the transition from childhood to adolescence, capturing the bittersweet nature of growing up and the importance of holding onto wonder and friendship.

**The Entertainment Industry Critique:** Through the villain Neppo and "The Space Show," the film offers a satirical commentary on the entertainment industry and celebrity culture. Neppo's desire to become a god through entertainment reflects anxieties about fame, audience consumption, and the commodification of wonder. The film suggests that true magic lies in genuine human connection rather than manufactured spectacle.

**Anime Tradition and Grand Adventure:** Welcome to the Space Show pays homage to classic space adventure anime while updating the genre for a new generation. The film channels the spirit of works like Galaxy Express 999 and Space Battleship Yamato while creating its own unique vision. Its epic scale—spanning multiple planets and alien civilizations—demonstrates the ambition of Japanese animation at its best.

**Family and Belonging:** The theme of finding one's way home resonates throughout the film. The children's ultimate goal is returning to Earth and their families, emphasizing that no matter how grand the adventure, home and family remain central to human experience. Pochi himself is a professor who has forgotten his responsibilities in his wanderings, adding another layer to this theme.
