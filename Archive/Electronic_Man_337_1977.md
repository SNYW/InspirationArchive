## The Pitch

Electronic Man 337 is a 1977 South Korean animated science fiction film that represents an ambitious entry in the early history of Korean animation. Produced by Samdo Film, the 63-minute film was released on December 8, 1977, directed by Im Jeong-gyu with a screenplay by Ji Sang-hak. The film marked an attempt to create a Korean answer to the giant robot superhero genre that had gained popularity through Japanese anime, particularly featuring a super-powered robot protagonist reminiscent of tokusatsu and anime heroes of the era.

The film was produced with a substantial budget of 33.7 billion won (a significant sum for its time), positioning it as one of the more ambitious Korean animated productions of the 1970s. It was intended as a follow-up to the success of Robot Taekwon V and incorporated design elements from Taekwon Kids Maruchi and Arachi, reflecting the Korean animation industry's practice of building on established mechanical designs and character archetypes.

## The Truth

The plot centers on world-renowned scientist Dr. Jang Dong-han, who invests 33.7 billion won to create a superpowered miniature robot named "Electronic Man 337" (전자인간 337). The robot possesses extraordinary abilities, including the capacity to hear all sounds occurring within 50,000 cubic meters—a hypersensitive auditory capability that serves as its primary super power.

Electronic Man 337 was conceived as part of Korea's nascent animated superhero genre, following in the footsteps of earlier works like Robot Taekwon V (1976). The film aimed to capture the growing appetite for robot superheroes among Korean children during the 1970s, a phenomenon largely driven by imported Japanese anime and tokusatsu series. The character's design and abilities reflect the influence of Western superhero properties as well, with references to characters like Superman, Spider-Man, and The Six Million Dollar Man—elements that have drawn criticism for perceived plagiarism in subsequent decades.

The film ran for 63 minutes and was released in Korean, representing a product of the Korean animation industry's experimental phase in the late 1970s. At this period, Korean animation was still developing its own identity, often working within the constraints of limited budgets while drawing inspiration from both Japanese and Western source material.

## The Core

Electronic Man 337 represents a fascinating artifact of Korean animation history during a period of rapid industrial growth and cultural transition in the 1970s. The film embodies the aspirations of Korea's animation industry to create homegrown superhero content capable of competing with imported Japanese media, which dominated the market at the time.

The choice of a miniature robot hero—rather than a giant robot—distinguishes Electronic Man 337 from contemporaries like Robot Taekwon V. This smaller scale may reflect production constraints, or alternatively, a desire to differentiate the character from the increasingly crowded giant robot genre. The robot's hypersensitive hearing ability suggests an attempt to create a unique power set rather than simply emulating existing superhero formulas.

The film's historical significance extends beyond its content to its position within the broader trajectory of Korean media. The 1970s marked a crucial period in Korean animation, with productions like this one laying groundwork for later developments in the industry. While Electronic Man 337 has faced criticism for derivative elements, it remains an important document of early Korean animated filmmaking and the country's efforts to develop a domestic superhero tradition during a period when imported content dominated.
