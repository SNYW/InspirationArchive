# Przekładaniec (1968)

## The Pitch

Przekładaniec (literally "Layer Cake" or "Sandwich") is a 1968 Polish short science fiction comedy film directed by Andrzej Wajda, based on a screenplay by renowned sci-fi author Stanisław Lem. This was Wajda's first comedy film, his first television production, and his only venture into science fiction—a remarkable collaboration between two of Poland's greatest artistic voices.

The film explores the thorny questions of identity and legal personhood through the lens of organ transplantation. When a rally driver named Richard Fox accidentally injures his brother Thomas in a race, surgeons transplant portions of Thomas's body into Richard. The resulting identity crisis sparks a tragicomedy involving insurance companies, lawyers, and existential questions about what makes a person who they are. Lem himself declared this the only film adaptation of his work that completely satisfied him—a rare endorsement from the notoriously critical author.

## The Truth

The story follows two brothers: Richard Fox, a rally racer, and his brother Thomas. During a rally race, Richard badly injures Thomas in a crash. The surgeon transplants 48.5% of Thomas's body into Richard, creating a legal and personal nightmare.

The insurance company refuses to pay Thomas's benefits because he is "incompletely deceased." Richard's wife demands he either pay for Thomas's care or recognize himself as Thomas and "rejoin the family." The family's lawyer is of no help. At the next rally, Richard accidentally crashes into his sister-in-law, two women, and a dog.

After a third catastrophic rally, the lawyer attempts to explain that Richard hasn't managed to accomplish anything yet—but it turns out Richard is no longer Richard at all. He is actually his co-driver, who survived with transplants from Richard's body parts. The film poses the question: when so much of a person's body has been replaced, are they still the same person? Can they own property? Can they be sued? Can they love?

The film premiered on August 17, 1968, on Polish Television. It was Wajda's first comedy and his only science fiction work, based on Lem's 1955 short story "Czy pan istnieje, Mr. Johns?" (Are you there, Mr. Jones?), which was also adapted as a radio play.

## The Core

The film tackles profound philosophical questions about identity through the absurdist lens of dark comedy. Drawing on themes of the Ship of Theseus and the Paradox of the Heap, Przekładaniec asks: at what point does physical transformation equal existential transformation? If a person becomes mostly machine or mostly transplanted organs, do they retain their original identity, rights, and responsibilities?

Wajda frames the dilemma in the domain of advanced prosthetics bordering on robotics, exploring themes that would later become central to transhumanist discourse. The cybernetic company that provides Richard's prostheses eventually sues him for return of all devices when he cannot pay his mounting debt—treating him as property rather than person. The court must decide: if Jones is machine, he cannot be sued; if he is still a person, he cannot become company property.

What makes Przekładaniec remarkable is its dual status as both philosophical thought experiment and entertainment. The film won the "Golden Screen" Award from Ekran magazine and received special recognition at the 1970 Sitges Film Festival. It represents a unique moment in Polish cinema when two giants—Wajda and Lem—collaborated to create something neither could have made alone. Lem wrote that he found the film's course of events to make more sense than his original story—a rare praise from the author known for his scathing critiques of adaptations.
