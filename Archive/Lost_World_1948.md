# Lost World (1948)

## The Pitch

Lost World is a pioneering science fiction manga created by Osamu Tezuka in 1948, making it one of the earliest examples of Japanese science fiction comics. Part of what is known as Tezuka's early science fiction trilogy (alongside Metropolis and Nextworld), this work predates much of what would later define the genre in manga and anime. The story follows a team of scientists who discover that a mysterious planet named Mamango is approaching Earth—bearing an uncanny resemblance to ancient Earth and inhabited by dinosaurs. What begins as a routine scientific expedition becomes an adventure involving stowaway criminals, ancient creatures, and the mysteries of a planet that broke away from Earth millions of years ago.

## The Truth

A new planet begins approaching Earth, and scientists know nothing about it. A team is assembled to venture to the mysterious planet—named Mamango—to discover its inhabitants and conditions. The expedition includes detective Shunsaku Ban, who is investigating a murder linked to Planet Mamango; young Kenichi Shikishima, who discovers the powerful potential of rocks from Planet Mamango; Captain Makeru Butamo, piloting the rocket ship; and the criminal Sekken Kao, who seeks the power of the alien world.

Upon arrival, the team discovers that Mamango is inhabited by dinosaurs and bears a striking resemblance to Earth from millions of years ago. The planet, which broke off from Earth approximately 5 million years ago, has returned for the first time since its separation. The scientists soon realize that criminals have stowed away on their spaceship and have come to Mamango for their own devious purposes. The story unfolds as the explorers must deal with both the prehistoric dangers of the dinosaur-inhabited world and the threat posed by the stowaway criminals.

The manga features several characters modeled after American cartoon characters, including Doctor Jupiter (modeled after Popeye), and appearances by Dagwood and Mickey Mouse—reflecting Tezuka's early influences from Western animation.

## The Core

Lost World represents a foundational moment in the history of Japanese manga and anime. As one of Osamu Tezuka's earliest works, it established many themes and visual storytelling techniques that would become hallmarks of his career and the manga industry as a whole. The manga demonstrates Tezuka's ambitious approach to science fiction, combining adventure, mystery, and speculative science in a way that was innovative for its time.

The story explores themes of planetary exploration and first contact, treating the alien world not as a threat but as a mirror to Earth—a reflection of our own past and a warning about the future. The presence of dinosaurs connects to timeless fascination with prehistoric life, while the criminal subplot adds moral complexity to what might otherwise be a straightforward adventure tale.

Tezuka's visual style, even in this early work, shows the dynamic storytelling that would earn him the title "God of Manga." The combination of science fiction elements with adventure storytelling set a template that would influence countless creators in the decades that followed. Lost World remains significant as a historical artifact demonstrating the origins of Japanese science fiction manga and the birth of a medium that would later produce globally influential works like Astro Boy, Akira, and Ghost in the Shell.
