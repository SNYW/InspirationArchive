## The Pitch

Treasure Planet is a 1982 Bulgarian animated science fiction film and the first full-length animated feature produced in Bulgaria. Directed by Rumen Petkov and produced by the Sofia Animation Studio, this 62-minute film is a bold sci-fi adaptation of Robert Louis Stevenson's classic adventure novel "Treasure Island." Set in a distant future where Earth has become entirely artificial and nature no longer exists, the story follows young Filipe as he embarks on a cosmic quest for Captain Flint's legendary treasure. Notably, this Bulgarian adaptation predates Disney's CGI version of "Treasure Planet" by exactly 20 years, making it a pioneering work in Eastern European animation that reimagines the pirate adventure genre for a spacefaring context.

## The Truth

The film opens with a framing device: Filipe narrates his adventure from inside a black hole. On a future Earth where nature has long vanished, a bio-robot named Black Dog confronts the space pirate Billy Bones over a star map leading to Captain Flint's treasure. After a series of comedic and tense encounters involving the dreaded "Black Spot," Billy hands over the map to Filipe before being killed.

Filipe delivers the map to Commander Smollett, who assembles a crew for the voyage aboard the spaceship Hispaniola. Among the crew is the one-legged Long John Silver and his crew of robots—mechanical beings with distinct personalities. During the journey, Silver reveals his plan to steal the map and claim the treasure to become "master of the whole universe."

When the crew lands on Treasure Planet, Silver kidnaps Filipe, but the young hero and Commander Smollett escape and follow the map's instructions. They confront illusions, melt the ghost of Captain Flint, and discover that the treasure is actually Flint's "Noah's Ark"—a repository containing samples of Earth's extinct flora and fauna. Silver escapes with the treasure, leaving Filipe and Smollett marooned.

However, Black Dog returns having overcome Silver's programming out of gratitude for Filipe treating him as more than a tool. The crew returns to Earth, bringing with them the precious biological samples—revealing that the true treasure was not gold, but the preservation of life itself.

## The Core

This film represents a significant milestone in Bulgarian cinema as the nation's first full-length animated feature, showcasing the creative ambitions of the Sofia Animation Studio during the communist era. The adaptation of Treasure Island into a space opera demonstrates how Eastern Bloc animators interpreted Western literary classics through a science fiction lens, creating unique hybrids that appealed to both domestic and international audiences.

The film's themes explore identity and humanity in a technological age—the robots aboard the Hispaniola display genuine emotional depth, and Black Dog's arc particularly emphasizes that sentience and compassion can emerge even in artificial beings. The reinterpretation of treasure as biological preservation rather than monetary wealth offers an ecological message about the value of nature and life.

The inclusion of the Bulgarian rock band Tangra's music anchors the film firmly in its 1980s Eastern European context, blending Western adventure narrative with local cultural production. The film's existence as a parallel to Disney's later adaptation—achieved without any connection to the later Hollywood project—demonstrates the universal appeal of the Treasure Island narrative when transposed into space.
