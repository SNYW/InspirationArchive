# Gorky 17 (1999)

**Tags:** 🌍 Poland | 🎭 Gritty | 📚 Military, Dystopia | 🧬 Aliens, Rebellion

**Medium:** Video Game
**Raw Score:** 3.9/5
**Total Ratings:** 2000
**Creator/Author:** Metropolis Software (Adrian Chmielarz, Andrzej Domański)

## The Pitch

Gorky 17 (known as Odium in North America) is a 1999 Polish tactical role-playing video game that blends turn-based combat with adventure game exploration. Set in a small Polish town near Lubin, the game puts players in command of NATO operatives investigating the mysterious appearance of hybrid creatures at a former Soviet military base. The player controls Cole Sullivan, a 40-year-old Canadian soldier with extensive scientific knowledge, who leads a commando team tasked with uncovering the truth behind the hybrids and finding missing members of the first reconnaissance group. The game features two distinct modes: real-time exploration using point-and-click mechanics, and turn-based tactical combat on grid-based maps.

## The Truth

The story begins with the sudden appearance of mysterious hybrid creatures in a former Soviet military base located in a small Polish town. NATO troops surround the area, and media from around the world converge on the location. The first group of operatives sent into the town vanishes without a trace. Cole Sullivan and his team are subsequently deployed to investigate the mystery, find the missing operatives, and determine the origin of the hybrids.

In the exploration mode, players navigate characters through the city environment, interacting with various objects and gathering information about the situation. Combat takes place in preset encounters in specific areas of each level. The combat system divides the screen into squares, with player and computer-controlled characters taking turns. Each character can move a certain number of squares and use a weapon once per turn, or choose to take cover. Different weapons have tactical effects—the pistol can only be fired orthogonally, while the rifle can be fired orthogonally and diagonally. The game ends if any player-controlled character dies.

Development began in 1997 under the working title "Haunted City." The game was rendered entirely using Real3D. The developers experimented with whether combat should be real-time, turn-based, or a hybrid system, ultimately choosing turn-based tactical combat. The PC version received above-average reviews, with critics praising the mix of genres, atmospheric environments, and fluid character animations, though some noted interface flaws and balance issues.

## The Core

Gorky 17 represents an important milestone in Polish game development, being one of the first notable tactical RPGs to emerge from Poland. The game combines elements of Polish history and geopolitics—specifically the legacy of Soviet military presence in Poland—with classic sci-fi tropes of alien encounters and military operations.

The game's themes explore the intersection of military conflict and unknown scientific phenomena, with the hybrid creatures serving as a catalyst for examining Cold War legacies and NATO cooperation. The setting in a small Polish town ground the cosmic mystery in mundane reality, creating an atmosphere of suburban unease beneath the sci-fi horror elements.

The tactical combat system, while derivative of earlier games like X-COM, offered its own innovations with the dual-mode gameplay structure. The game's Polish origins make it a significant entry in the history of Eastern European game development, showcasing the emerging capabilities of Polish studios in the late 1990s.
