# Demon City Shinjuku (1988)

**Medium:** Anime
**Raw Score:** 6.3
**Total Ratings:** 5000

**Creator/Author:** Directed by Yoshiaki Kawajiri; Based on the novel by Hideyuki Kikuchi; Studio: Madhouse

## The Pitch

In 1990s Tokyo, a catastrophic "Demon Quake" destroys Shinjuku, transforming it into a demon-haunted wasteland. Ten years later, the city remains a twisted netherworld where supernatural entities roam the ruined skyscrapers. When the World President is targeted by a curse, his daughter recruits Kyoya Izayoi—the son of a legendary hero who died fighting the demon lord Rebi Ra—to venture into the heart of darkness. What awaits is a surreal journey through a city where the boundary between human and monster has dissolved completely.

## The Truth

The story opens with the epic battle between former friends: the heroic Genichirou and his corrupted former ally Rebi Ra, who has allowed himself to be possessed by demonic forces to gain ultimate power. Rebi Ra summons a devastating earthquake that tears Shinjuku from the rest of Tokyo, transforming it into a demon realm. Genichirou is killed, and Shinjuku becomes a twisted wilderness of monsters and ruined architecture.

Ten years later, the World President—who was saved years ago by the hero Aguni Rai—is attacked by cursed plants. The truth emerges: Rebi Ra is using the President as a ritual sacrifice to fully open the gateway between Earth and the Demon Realm. The President has only three days to live unless Rebi Ra is defeated.

The President's daughter, Sayaka Rama, seeks out the only person who can help: Kyoya Izayoi, the son of Genichirou, who has inherited his father's supernatural powers. Initially reluctant, Kyoya is persuaded to enter Demon City Shinjuku after Sayaka pleads for his help.

Inside the ruined city, Kyoya encounters bizarre demons, surreal landscapes, and strange allies. He battles through various demonic forces, learning about his father's legacy and his own destiny. The city itself is a character—ruined buildings, neon signs flickering through the chaos, streets that lead to impossible places.

The climax reveals that Chiaki, Kyoya's friend, has been working with the demons, and Kyoya must face both the external threat of Rebi Ra and the internal betrayal. In the final confrontation, Kyoya uses his father's legacy and his own courage to defeat Rebi Ra, closing the portal and potentially saving both the President and the world.

The OVA ends with theDemon Quake reversed, Shinjuku returning to normal, and Kyoya becoming the new protector of Tokyo.

## The Core

Demon City Shinjuku is a fascinating fusion of cyberpunk urban decay and supernatural horror, exploring themes of identity, legacy, and the monstrous. The film poses uncomfortable questions about what makes a monster: is it appearance, or is it choices? The demons in Shinjuku are often more human in their motivations than their human adversaries.

The setting itself is a powerful metaphor for urban alienation and the loss of humanity in modern society. Shinjuku becomes a physical manifestation of the darkness that lurks beneath the surface of civilization—the part of the city we pretend doesn't exist.

The film also explores legacy and generational trauma. Kyoya must come to terms with his father's legacy while forging his own identity. He's not simply repeating his father's heroic path but finding his own way.

Visually, the film is a stunning example of late-80s anime artistry. The contrast between the gleaming modern Tokyo and the ruined demon city creates a powerful visual metaphor. The action sequences are choreographed with precision, and the demon designs are genuinely unsettling.

The film was notably influential—the opening fight scene was reused in the 1995 cyberpunk film Johnny Mnemonic. However, the OVA remains relatively unknown outside of anime collector circles, making it a true hidden gem of the cyberpunk genre.
