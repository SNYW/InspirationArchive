## The Pitch

The X from Outer Space is a 1967 Japanese kaiju film that tells the story of the spaceship AAB Gamma, dispatched from Japan to investigate reports of UFOs near Mars. When the crew encounters one of the mysterious spacecraft, it sprays their ship with alien spores. A sample is brought back to Earth, where one of the spores accidentally exposed to acid grows into a giant monster named "Guilala." The creature feeds on energy sources, grows increasingly powerful, and begins destroying Tokyo. The military struggles against the beast until fighter jets coat it in "Guilalanium"—a substance that prevents energy absorption—shrinking it back to its spore form. The government launches the harmless spore into space to orbit the Sun forever.

## The Truth

The film opens with the AAB Gamma launching from Japan on a mission to investigate UFOs reported near Mars. The crew, led by Captain Sano, includes Dr. Kato and other scientists. Upon reaching Mars, they encounter a UFO that suddenly attacks, spraying the spaceship with glowing spores. Despite damage, the crew manages to return to Earth with a sample.

Back on Earth, the spores are studied at a research facility. One spore is accidentally exposed to acid during an experiment and rapidly grows into a massive creature—the monster Guilala. The beast escapes and begins its rampage through Tokyo, feeding on various energy sources and growing larger and more powerful with each consumption. Guilala spits fireballs, devours nuclear fuel, and transforms into a flaming orb to travel vast distances in minutes, destroying all military opposition.

The Japan Self-Defense Forces mobilize but conventional weapons prove useless. Finally, the military develops "Guilalanium," a special substance that blocks energy absorption. Fighter jets loaded with Guilalanium bombs successfully coat the monster, causing it to shrink back down to its original spore form. The harmless spore is placed in a glass container and launched into space, where it will orbit the Sun indefinitely.

## The Core

The X from Outer Space stands as a quintessential example of 1960s Japanese kaiju cinema, embodying the genre's blend of science fiction adventure and practical monster effects. Directed by Kazui Nihonmatsu for Shochiku, the film is noted for its unusually elaborate scientific plot for a monster movie—complete with space travel, alien microbiology, and energy-based biology—compared to typical kaiju entries of the era.

The film has achieved cult status as one of the "silliest" and most beloved rubber-suit monster movies, with critics describing Guilala as a "20-story chicken with a head shaped like a jet plane." Its harebrained kaiju epic quality, combined with an unpredictable soundtrack mixing 1960s pop and bossa nova, has made it a favorite for "stoned best-bad-movie" viewing.

Film historian Chuck Stephens described it as having "a well-deserved reputation as one of the silliest and, as a consequence, most beloved rubber-suit monster movies ever made." The Criterion Collection released the film on DVD as part of their Eclipse series "When Horror Came to Shochiku," recognizing its cult significance within the tokusatsu genre.

The film is notable for being never theatrically released in the United States, instead airing on American television in 1968 via American International Television—making it a relatively obscure entry in the kaiju canon that has only gained recognition through home video and critical reevaluation.
