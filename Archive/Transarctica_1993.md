# Transarctica (1993)

## The Pitch

Transarctica (also known as Arctic Baron) is a 1993 French post-apocalyptic action-adventure and strategy video game developed by Silmarils. Set in a future Earth devastated by the greenhouse effect gone wrong, the planet is now entirely covered in thick clouds, with giant wolf packs roaming the frozen wastes and mammoths having re-emerged from elephant stock. Humanity survives in scattered settlements connected by a network of massive armored trains controlled by the ruthless Viking Union. Players hijack the Transarctica train and set out on a desperate quest to find the "sun" in this frozen wasteland.

## The Truth

In the 21st century, humanity attempts to counteract the devastating greenhouse effect with a last-ditch geoengineering effort—but it works far too well. Centuries later, the Earth is encased in perpetual darkness beneath an opaque cloud layer. Giant wolf packs hunt across the frozen plains, and the mammoth has returned from extinction. Humanity clings to survival in isolated settlements connected by a network of massive armored trains.

The Viking Union, a powerful organization, controls the train network with an iron fist, maintaining power through sheer military might. A small group of radicals manages to hijack the Transarctica train and sets out across Eurasia and North Africa in search of the legendary "sun"—a mythical place where the clouds part and warmth and light might exist once again.

The game combines exploration and combat modes. Players navigate the train across the frozen landscape, managing coal as both currency and fuel, visiting towns to trade and upgrade the train, gathering clues about the quest. Combat involves real-time train-to-train battles using cannons, machine guns, infantry, and mammoth cavalry. Combat is won when enemies are disarmed, allowing the player to loot intact wagons and materials.

The setting draws inspiration from the post-apocalyptic novel series "La Compagnie des glaces" (The Ice Company) by French writer Georges-Jean Arnaud.

## The Core

Transarctica represents a distinctive branch of post-apocalyptic storytelling centered not on scarcity or radiation, but on climate catastrophe and frozen darkness. The greenhouse effect backfired catastrophically, creating an "ice apocalypse" where the solution to global warming became worse than the problem. This inverts typical climate fiction tropes—the enemy is not heat but unending cold and the loss of the sun.

The game presents a harsh dystopian world where the Viking Union maintains order through domination rather than cooperation. Their control of transportation infrastructure translates directly to power, and resistance means operating outside their system. The player becomes an outlaw seeking not just survival but hope—literally searching for the sun.

The use of armored trains as the primary setting and vehicle is unique in post-apocalyptic fiction, later echoed in works like the graphic novel and film "Snowpiercer." The train becomes both shelter and weapon, a mobile community in a world where fixed settlements are vulnerable to the dangers outside.

While criticized for its cumbersome interface and slow combat, Transarctica offers an original and atmospheric premise that combines environmental commentary with survivalist adventure. Its French origins also represent a piece of European science fiction gaming history from an era when French developers like Silmarils were creating distinctive titles in the adventure and strategy genres.
