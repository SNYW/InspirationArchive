## The Pitch

Kokey is a 1997 Filipino science fiction film produced by Star Cinema, one of the Philippines' most prominent film studios. The film tells the story of two siblings, Bong and Anna, who discover an extraterrestrial being whose spaceship has crashed on Earth. Rather than fearing the alien, the children befriend Kokey and hide him from their parents, eventually forming a deep bond with the gentle alien. Kokey uses his extraterrestrial powers to help the family's small business, attracting customers and bringing prosperity. However, Kokey becomes increasingly homesick, longing to return to his own planet and family. The film explores themes of friendship, compassion, and the pain of separation, set against the backdrop of a typical Filipino family struggling to make ends meet.

## The Truth

On their way home from school, siblings Bong and Anna stumble upon a crashed spaceship in an abandoned lot. They discover Kokey, a young alien whose vessel has been destroyed upon landing on Earth. The children decide to keep Kokey a secret from their parents, hiding him in their modest home. At first, Kokey's presence is a source of wonder and excitement—his extraterrestrial abilities include telekinesis and the power to generate light, which the children use to create a successful food stall business that attracts numerous customers.

As time passes, the family grows to love Kokey as one of their own. The alien becomes an integral part of their household, helping with daily chores and bringing joy to everyone. However, Kokey begins to show signs of deep sadness. He frequently points to the sky, shedding silent tears as he longs for his parents and his home planet. Despite the children's attempts to help him contact his family, their efforts prove unsuccessful.

The film culminates in an emotional decision as the family must come to terms with letting Kokey go. The children's love for Kokey compels them to help him find a way back home, even though it means saying goodbye to their beloved alien friend. The resolution emphasizes the selflessness of true friendship and the universal desire to be reunited with one's family.

## The Core

Kokey represents a significant entry in Philippine cinema's engagement with the science fiction genre, particularly in its treatment of extraterrestrial life. The film stands out for its humanistic approach to the alien encounter narrative, shifting the focus from conflict and fear to compassion and connection. Rather than portraying the alien as a threat or a source of danger, Kokey presents an extraterrestrial being as a vulnerable entity in need of kindness—a reflection of Filipino cultural values regarding hospitality and care for others.

The film also serves as a family drama wrapped in science fiction elements. The struggles of the family—financial hardship, the need for community support, and the bonds between siblings—ground the fantastical premise in recognizable Filipino experiences. Kokey's powers becoming a source of economic relief for the family speaks to broader themes of hope and upward mobility.

In terms of its place in Asian science fiction cinema, Kokey aligns with a tradition of family-friendly sci-fi that treats alien contact as an opportunity for moral growth rather than technological or military conflict. The film's optimistic tone and focus on emotional resonance over action distinguishes it from typical Western science fiction narratives of the era, offering a uniquely Filipino perspective on universal themes of belonging and home.
