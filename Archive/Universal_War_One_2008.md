# Universal War One (2008)

**Tags:** 🌍 France | 🎭 Gritty | 📚 Military, Space-Opera, Time-Travel | 🧬 Redemption, War, Humanity's Future

**Medium:** Graphic Novel
**Raw Score:** 3.5/5
**Total Ratings:** 100

**Creator/Author:** Denis Bajram

## The Pitch

In the not-so-distant future, humanity has colonized the outer reaches of the solar system. But when a mysterious, incomprehensible black wall appears near Saturn—absorbing all light and matter and cutting off access to the outer planets—tensions between Earth and the colonization companies explode into all-out civil war. Enter the Purgatory Squadron: a band of soldiers given a second chance after courts-martial for crimes ranging from cowardice to rape. Led by the principled Captain June Williamson, this ragtag group of criminals and outcasts must investigate the black wall before the war destroys humanity's future among the stars.

## The Truth

The story begins with Captain June Williamson protecting civilians—women and children, including families of striking miners on Titan—from being massacred by her own commanding officer during the mining riots. Her act of defiance earns her a command of the Purgatory Squadron: a unit composed entirely of soldiers facing court-martial for serious crimes.

The squadron includes: Lt. Kate Von Richtburg (daughter of Admiral Von Richtburg), Paulo "Mario" Del Gado (a navigator who panicked and destroyed three supply ships), John "Balti" Baltimore (a would-be hero who killed twelve people in a rescue mishap), Amina El Moudden (who castrated a colonel who tried to rape her), Lt. Colonel Edward "Ed" Kalish (a genius physicist who beat twelve people senseless in a bar fight), and Milorad Racunicsa (a rapist).

When the mysterious black wall appears near Saturn, cutting off the outer colonies, Admiral Von Richtburg—having discovered the Colonization Industrial Companies (CIC) started the war to seize power—orders Purgatory Squadron to investigate. They devise a method to breach the wall but are fired upon by an unknown vessel. The UEF 3rd Fleet follows them through, beginning the First Universal War.

Through the course of the story, the squadron experiences time dilation due to their proximity to the wall. Balti is killed as a result of these temporal effects. Ed Kalish discovers the true nature of the wall—that it appears to be some kind of temporal or dimensional barrier. The story culminates in revelations about the wall's origins and the beginning of humanity's understanding of this cosmic phenomenon.

A sequel series, Universal War Two, continues the story, exploring what happened "40 Years in the Desert."

## The Core

Universal War One is a seminal work of military science fiction that explores several profound themes. At its core, it's about redemption—can people who have committed terrible acts prove themselves worthy? The Purgatory Squadron is literally a group of condemned soldiers given one chance to earn their humanity back.

The comic also examines the nature of conflict itself. The civil war between United Earth Forces and the Colonization Industrial Companies is revealed to have been engineered deliberately—triggered by the appearance of the wall—which raises questions about who really controls humanity's expansion into space.

Most intriguingly, the black wall serves as a metaphor for the unknown frontiers that await humanity. It's described as incomprehensible—absorbing light and matter—and represents the ultimate unknown that drives human conflict even as we reach for the stars.

Denis Bajram's artwork is visually stunning, with detailed spacecraft designs and dramatic battle sequences that influenced subsequent science fiction films. The story's blend of hard military SF with philosophical questions about human nature and redemption makes it a standout in the genre.

The time dilation and time travel elements add a layer of complexity, exploring how human perception changes when confronted with cosmic-scale phenomena. It's a meditation on humanity's place in the universe—small, flawed, but capable of extraordinary things when given the chance.
