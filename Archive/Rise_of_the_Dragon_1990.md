# Rise of the Dragon (1990)

**Tags:** 🌍 USA | 🎭 Gritty | 📚 Cyberpunk, Dystopia | 🧬 Megacorps, VR

**Medium:** Video Game
**Raw Score:** 4.15/5
**Total Ratings:** 71
**Creator/Author:** Dynamix / Sierra On-Line

## The Pitch

Rise of the Dragon is a 1990 graphic adventure game that marked Dynamix's venture into the cyberpunk genre. Set in a dystopian Los Angeles of 2053, players take on the role of William "Blade" Hunter, a former LAPD officer turned private detective. When the mayor's rebellious daughter Chandra is found dead and horribly mutated after experimenting with a new designer drug called MTZ, Blade is called upon to track down those responsible. The game combines detective work, strategy puzzles, and action sequences, featuring a time-management system where players must complete their investigation within three in-game days or lose. Originally released for DOS, it was later ported to Amiga, Mac OS, and Sega CD.

## The Truth

In 2053 Los Angeles, detective William "Blade" Hunter is hired to investigate the death of Chandra, the mayor's daughter, who has been mutated by a dangerous new designer drug called MTZ. Blade's investigation leads him to contact Chandra's streetwise friend "the Jake," who directs him to Chen Lu, the dealer who supplied the lethal drug.

When Blade arrives at Lu's apartment, he discovers Lu has been assassinated with an overdose of MTZ—punishment from his employers for attracting public attention by causing a politician daughter's death. As Blade digs deeper, he enlists help from his girlfriend Karyn at the Crime Records Bureau to track down Lu's accomplices and uncover the mutagenic nature of MTZ.

The trail leads Blade to discover the MTZ business is part of an underground Chinese Mafia operation led by Deng Hwang, a megalomaniacal drug kingpin who plans world domination through poisoning the Hollywood reservoir with MTZ. Blade must disrupt the drug operations, rescue the kidnapped Jake, and ultimately infiltrate Hwang's headquarters to rescue Karyn and confront Hwang himself—who transforms into the dragon Bahumat in a climactic final battle.

The game features multiple endings depending on player choices: whether Karyn survives, and whether Blade accepts the mayor's gratitude or rejects him.

## The Core

Rise of the Dragon stands as an underrated classic of early cyberpunk gaming that predated many later genre staples. The game effectively captured the cyberpunk atmosphere with its dystopian near-future Los Angeles, complete with corporate crime, street-level investigations, and body modification themes. Critics noted its visual detail and non-linear approach to puzzle-solving, where player choices directly influenced the storyline and character relationships.

The game won a Special Award for Artistic Achievement from Computer Gaming World in 1991 and was later ranked among the best and most innovative games of its era. Notably, it pioneered the use of dynamic hot-spotted maps as game world travel interfaces. The Sega CD version introduced voice acting, making it one of the earlier CD-ROM adventures with full voice work.

What makes Rise of the Dragon significant is its blend of noir detective fiction with cyberpunk worldbuilding—a combination that influenced later games in the genre. Its time-pressure mechanics added urgency to the investigation, while multiple puzzle solutions and character memory of past player actions created meaningful replay value. Computer Gaming World noted it more effectively depicted cyberpunk atmosphere than many contemporary games, earning praise for its story, interface, and optional action sequences.
