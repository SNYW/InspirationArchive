## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)

Battle Angel (known in Japan as Gunnm) is a 1993 Japanese original video animation (OVA) that serves as a compressed adaptation of the first two volumes of Yukito Kishiro's influential cyberpunk manga series. Produced by Madhouse, the OVA consists of two episodes—"Rusty Angel" and "Tears Sign"—that introduce audiences to the dark, rain-soaked world of Scrap Iron City and its cyborg inhabitants. The story follows Gally, a amnesiac cyborg found in the垃圾堆 (dump heap) by the cyberphysician Daisuke Ido, who reconstructs her into a powerful warrior body. What follows is a visceral exploration of identity, humanity, and the desperate pursuit of dreams in a stratified future where the wealthy live in a floating city above while the rest scrap survive in the ruins below.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

The OVA is set in a future where humanity has colonized the underside of a massive floating city called Zalem (known as Tiphares in some translations). Below lies Scrap Iron City—a brutal, lawless wasteland where cyborgs and humans coexist in uneasy harmony, and where hunter-warriors make their living tracking down bounties.

The story begins with Daisuke Ido discovering the remains of a female cyborg in the垃圾dump heap that serves as Scrap Iron City's dumping ground. Despite her deteriorated state, he recognizes she is still alive and takes her home, transplanting her into a new body—a powerful combat frame later identified as the Berserker Body. He names her Gally.

Shortly after her recovery, Gally meets Yugo, a young orphan who works as a maintenance helper for Ido. Yugo dreams of one day reaching Zalem, the floating city above, believing that if he can accumulate enough money, he can buy passage to a better life. Gally becomes drawn to Yugo, and the two form a connection that will define the OVA's emotional core.

When Gally witnesses Ido's skills as a hunter-warrior—a bounty hunter who tracks down dangerous criminals—she becomes intrigued by the profession. Meanwhile, the criminal element of Scrap Iron City grows restless. A cyborg named Grewcica, seeking revenge against Ido and Gally, is rebuilt by the vengeful Chiren, a former lover of Ido who dreams of returning to Zalem.

The narrative escalates as Gally registers as a hunter-warrior herself, going on a relentless bounty spree to earn money for Yugo's dream. However, the truth about Zalem is darker than Yugo realizes: passage to the floating city is a lie perpetuated by the criminal overlord Vector, who controls the resource tubes connecting Scrap Iron City to Zalem. Those who attempt to climb the tubes are inevitably destroyed by the defense system.

The OVA builds to a tragic climax: Yugo discovers the truth, attempts to climb the tube anyway, and is killed by the city's defense systems. Gally manages to catch him, but he slips from her grasp, leaving her clutching his severed arm as he falls. In the aftermath, Gally and Ido release a balloon carrying Yugo's arm and Chiren's earring toward Zalem—a poignant final image of unreachable dreams.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)

**The Body as Battleground:** Battle Angel presents a world where the boundary between human and machine has completely dissolved. Gally's entire existence is defined by her cyborg body—she is literally rebuilt from salvaged parts, her identity tied to her mechanical form. The OVA explores what it means to be human when your body is no longer flesh, when your memories could be artificial, when your emotions might be programmed responses. This theme of technological embodiment predates and influenced later works like Ghost in the Shell.

**The Floating City as Class Metaphor:** Zalem represents the ultimate expression of class stratification—a paradise for the wealthy that casts its shadow over the suffering masses below. The impossibility of reaching Zalem (the tubes, the defense systems, the lies) serves as a metaphor for social mobility in a world where the elite have built walls even in the sky. Yugo's dream of climbing to Zalem is both aspirational and tragically naive.

**Memory and Identity:** Gally's amnesia is central to her character—she has no recollection of her past, no understanding of why she was in the dump heap, no knowledge of her combat abilities until they manifest. The OVA asks whether identity is determined by memory or by choice, whether Gally is defined by what she remembers or what she does.

**The Price of Dreams:** Yugo's obsession with reaching Zalem drives much of the plot, and his ultimate fate serves as a cautionary tale about dreams that consume everything. The OVA suggests that hope can be both motivating and destructive, that the desire to escape one's reality can lead to tragedy.

**Hunter-Warrior Culture:** The bounty hunter profession represents a freelance justice system in a world where traditional law enforcement has failed. Gally's transformation into a hunter-warrior shows her adapting to her world's brutal logic, using violence as a means of survival and purpose.

The OVA's influence is substantial—it directly inspired James Cameron's 2019 film Alita: Battle Angel and helped establish many cyberpunk anime tropes that would become genre standards. While the manga would eventually expand into the massive Battle Angel Alita franchise, the 1993 OVA remains a compelling, self-contained exploration of its themes.
