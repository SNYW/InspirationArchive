## The Pitch

Starship Troopers is a Japanese six-part OVA produced by Sunrise and Bandai, released in 1988. It is based on Robert A. Heinlein's controversial 1959 military science fiction novel. The OVA was dedicated to Heinlein, who died before the first part was released. The story follows Johnny Rico, an ordinary high school student who enlists in the military after learning that his classmate Carmencita plans to join upon graduation. Set in a future where humanity battles an unidentified alien species known as "bugs," the series explores Rico's transformation from civilian to soldier through the brutal realities of military training and combat.

## The Truth

The story begins with Johnny Rico, a typical high school student in the Earth Federation, deciding to enlist in the military after his classmate Carmencita Ibañez announces her plans to join upon graduation. His best friend Carl reveals that Earth is at war with an alien species, encouraging Rico to enlist despite his parents' objections.

During basic training, Rico experiences the harsh realities of military life. He witnesses fellow recruits being dismissed, faces severe punishment for disobeying orders—even when attempting to save allies—and suffers the devastating loss of his mother when the Bugs attack his home city. As a platoon leader in simulated exercises, Rico is unexpectedly thrust into actual combat with alien forces. Many of his comrades are killed, but Rico survives and earns recognition as a soldier.

After the battle, Rico reunites with Carmencita, but tensions arise between the military and civilian populations, as well as friction between the army and navy. When Carmencita's ship collides with an alien vessel and is destroyed, she is presumed dead. Rico then participates in a major assault on the alien homeworld, Kurendatsu, where he confronts both the loss of friends and the shocking realization that the enemy also fights for survival.

Following prolonged battle, Rico discovers that Carmencita has survived and is recovering in a military hospital. The story concludes with Rico finally confessing his long-suppressed feelings for her.

## The Core

The Starship Troopers OVA represents a significant adaptation of Heinlein's influential novel, translating its themes of citizenship, military service, and moral philosophy into visual anime form. The series examines the bond between soldiers, the cost of war, and questions of heroism and duty. Unlike the more controversial elements of Heinlein's novel—which some critics interpreted as fascist or militaristic—the OVA focuses more directly on the personal journey of Johnny Rico and the human cost of interstellar conflict.

The animation showcases impressive mechanical designs and battle sequences for its era, while the narrative provides a more accessible entry point to Heinlein's complex themes. The revelation that the alien "bugs" are not simply mindless monsters but a civilization fighting for their own survival adds philosophical depth to what could otherwise be a straightforward military action series.

The OVA remains notable as an early example of anime adapting Western science fiction literature, bridging Japanese animation and American military SF traditions.
