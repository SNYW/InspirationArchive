# Near Dark (1987)

## The Pitch

A young Oklahoma man meets a mysterious drifter named Mae, only to discover she's a vampire who turns him into one of the undead. Forced to join a nomadic family of bloodthirsty vampires traveling across the American Southwest, he must navigate his new existence while trying to find a cure before he loses his humanity forever. This neo-Western vampire tale mixes the grit of biker films with the loneliness of frontier mythology, creating a haunting meditation on addiction, family, and what it means to truly live.

## The Truth

Caleb Colton, a young man living in a small Oklahoma town, meets Mae, an attractive young drifter, at a local diner. Just before sunrise, she bites him on his neck and disappears. When the rising sun causes Caleb's flesh to smoke and burn, he realizes he's been turned into a vampire.

Mae returns with a group of roaming vampires traveling in an RV. The group's psychotic member, Severen, wants to kill Caleb immediately, but their charismatic leader, Jesse Hooker, reluctantly agrees to let Caleb stay with them for a week to prove he can learn to hunt and gain the group's trust.

Caleb struggles with his new nature—he's unwilling to kill humans to feed, which alienates him from the other vampires. To protect him, Mae kills humans for him and has him drink from her wrist. The group moves through various towns, leaving carnage in their wake, including a bar massacre where they kill all the patrons and set the building on fire.

After Caleb endangers the group by letting a bar survivor escape, most want to kill him. However, when Caleb helps them escape during a police raid at a motel—exposing himself to daylight to do so—Jesse and the others become grateful. Caleb learns that Jesse is approximately 150 years old, having "fought for the South" during the American Civil War.

Meanwhile, Caleb's father has been searching for the vampire group. A child vampire named Homer in the group takes an interest in Caleb's sister Sarah, wanting to turn her. During the confrontation, Caleb's father arrives holding the vampires at gunpoint. Jesse taunts him into shooting, then regurgitates the bullet. In the confusion, Sarah opens a door, letting in sunlight and forcing the vampires to retreat.

Caleb suggests a blood transfusion might reverse his vampirism. The experimental transfusion works—Caleb is cured. That night, the vampires return looking for revenge. Mae helps Caleb escape with Sarah, but the vampires kidnap his sister. Caleb gives chase on horseback, eventually confronting Severen. He commandeers a tractor-trailer and runs Severen over, but the vampire manages to rip apart the engine wiring, causing the truck to jackknife and explode, killing Severen.

Jesse and his girlfriend Diamondback pursue Caleb and Sarah as dawn breaks. Mae breaks free from the group to help Caleb, though her flesh begins to burn from the sun. She carries Sarah to safety under Caleb's jacket. The child vampire Homer follows them into the sunlight and dies. Jesse and Diamondback attempt to run Caleb and Sarah down but are forced to escape as the sun rises; they die when their car explodes.

Later, Mae awakens— she's also been given a transfusion and cured. Frightened and uncertain about their new humanity, Caleb embraces her as the film ends.

## The Core

Near Dark is a masterful genre hybrid that uses vampire mythology as a metaphor for addiction and alienation. The vampire family represents a toxic chosen family—a gang of nomads bound together by their need for blood, which parallels substance addiction. Caleb's struggle to resist killing mirrors the battle against addiction, and his eventual cure through transfusion suggests the possibility of redemption.

The Western genre elements are crucial: the vampires are modern-day frontier outlaws, roaming the American landscape in an RV rather than on horses. Jesse Hooker's claim to have fought in the Civil War connects the vampire mythology to America's violent history. The film presents vampirism not as supernatural evil but as a different mode of existence—one that requires violence to survive.

Kathryn Bigelow's direction creates a dreamlike atmosphere filled with striking imagery: the sun burning Caleb's flesh, the neon-lit motels, the blood-soaked encounters. The Tangerine Dream soundtrack adds to the surreal, liminal quality.

The film also explores themes of family—both biological and chosen. Caleb must choose between his biological family (his father and sister) and his vampire "family." The resolution suggests that genuine connection and love can transcend even the most monstrous transformations.

Near Dark helped revive serious vampire cinema in the late 1980s, paving the way for more nuanced portrayals of vampires in subsequent decades. Its influence can be seen in films like The Lost Boys (released just two months later) and later works that treat vampire mythology with greater depth and complexity.
