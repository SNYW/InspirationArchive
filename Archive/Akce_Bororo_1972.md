## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)

Akce Bororo (Operation Bororo) is a 1972 Czechoslovak science fiction film directed by Otakar Fuka. Set in Czechoslovakia, the film follows two alien visitors from the distant planet Tonatiu who arrive on Earth on a vital mission: to obtain a miraculous medicine from the Bororo Indian tribe, which they believe can save their dying civilization. The film blends elements of science fiction with adventure, as the aliens navigate Earth's landscape and form connections with the indigenous Bororo people. The story explores themes of cross-cultural encounter, sacrifice, and the determination to preserve one's species against seemingly impossible odds.

The film features notable performances from Božidara Turzonovová as the alien visitor Zuzana and Svatopluk Matyáš as her Earth-born companion. The scientific advisor for the film was Ivan Vojtěch Frič, adding authenticity to the Bororo cultural elements. Filming locations included the Velický waterfall in the High Tatras mountains and the ČSAV observatory in Ondřejov near Prague. The film represents a unique entry in Czechoslovak cinema, combining science fiction premises with folk elements and adventure storytelling.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

The story begins when two alien visitors, Ori-Ana and Seturi, arrive on Earth from their home planet Tonatiu aboard a spacecraft. Their civilization is facing a catastrophic crisis, and they have learned that the Bororo Indian tribe in South America possesses a miraculous medicine that could save their people. The two aliens are tasked with obtaining this medicine at any cost.

Upon landing on Earth, the aliens take on human appearances and begin their search for the Bororo people. Ori-Ana, portrayed by Božidara Turzonovová, adopts the name Zuzana and begins her journey through Czechoslovakia, eventually making her way to the Brazilian rainforest where the Bororo tribe resides. During her quest, she encounters various challenges and forms a connection with an Earth man who becomes her companion and helps her in her mission.

The aliens face numerous obstacles in their search, including the vast distance and cultural differences. Despite their best efforts, both Ori-Ana and Seturi ultimately perish during their mission on Earth. However, their sacrifice is not in vain—shortly after their deaths, their identical successors arrive on Earth, determined to complete the mission their predecessors started.

The film's narrative emphasizes the theme of perseverance and the willingness to sacrifice for one's civilization. The aliens' determination to save their people, even at the cost of their own lives, drives the entire story. The final act shows the new arrivals continuing the quest, demonstrating that the mission transcends any individual.
