## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)

Save the Green Planet! is a 2003 South Korean science fiction black comedy that tells the story of Lee Byeong-gu, a paranoid conspiracy theorist who becomes obsessed with the belief that extraterrestrials from the Andromeda Galaxy are planning to invade Earth. When his mother falls into a coma after being poisoned in a pharmaceutical drug trial, Byeong-gu convinces himself that the company's executive is actually an alien commander who can communicate with the Andromedans during an upcoming lunar eclipse. He kidnaps the executive and holds him hostage in his basement, subjecting him to bizarre tests to prove his alien origins.

The film was a catastrophic box office failure, earning only $15,516 in South Korea, and received limited theatrical release. However, it has since achieved cult classic status both domestically and internationally. Critics praise its unique blend of genres—combining elements of black comedy, psychological thriller, and science fiction—along with its biting satire of corporate greed and government conspiracy theories. The film won multiple awards including Best New Director at the Blue Dragon Film Awards and the Grand Bell Awards, and has gained a devoted following among fans of offbeat Korean cinema.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

Lee Byeong-gu is a socially isolated young man living in Seoul who works at a minor job and spends his free time obsessively researching conspiracy theories about extraterrestrials. He believes that aliens from "Andromeda PK 45" are secretly preparing to invade Earth, and that he is the only person who can stop them. His only companions are Su-ni, his childlike circus performer girlfriend, and his mother, who works as a cleaning lady.

When Byeong-gu's mother suddenly falls into a coma after being poisoned during a clinical drug trial conducted by a major pharmaceutical company, his existing paranoia spirals into full-blown delusion. He becomes convinced that the company's executive, Kang Man-shik, is actually the commander of the alien invasion force. With Su-ni's reluctant help, Byeong-gu abducts Kang from his car and imprisons him in a soundproof basement room in his apartment building.

In the basement, Byeong-gu subjects Kang to a series of bizarre tests to determine if he is truly an alien. These include scrubbing Kang with a Korean painkiller to see if his skin reacts differently, and using an "Italy towel" to check for any unusual physiological responses. Meanwhile, Kang gradually reveals that his company did indeed poison Byeong-gu's mother during an illegal drug trial—a connection between the conspiracy theory and real-world corporate corruption that blurs the line between Kang's sanity and Byeong-gu's paranoia.

When a detective begins investigating Kang's disappearance, Byeong-gu must hide all evidence of the kidnapping. However, the detective later discovers human remains in the area and confronts Byeong-gu, who kills him in a fit of paranoia. Meanwhile, Su-ni is killed by a robotic arm at Kang's factory when she goes to retrieve evidence. The police eventually locate the hideout, and during the confrontation, Kang claims he is truly an alien and that his company has been preparing for the invasion.

At the factory, Kang activates a robotic arm that kills Su-ni and injures Byeong-gu. When police arrive, they shoot Byeong-gu, who dies believing he has failed to save Earth. In the final twist, aliens arrive to retrieve Kang, revealing him to be their king. Disillusioned with humanity, Kang declares Earth a "failed experiment" and the aliens destroy the planet. The film ends with a video diary showing brief moments of happiness in Byeong-gu's life—revealing the tragic human behind the conspiracy theorist.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)

**Conspiracy Culture and Paranoia:** The film presents a world where corporate malfeasance and government secrecy have so eroded public trust that distinguishing between legitimate suspicion and paranoid delusion becomes impossible. Byeong-gu's conspiracy theories, while ultimately proven wrong in their specifics, are rooted in real corruption—he was right that the pharmaceutical company poisoned his mother, even if his alien narrative was false. The film suggests that in a world of actual conspiracies, the line between rational skepticism and pathological paranoia dissolves.

**Corporate Critique:** The pharmaceutical company represents unchecked corporate power operating without accountability. The illegal drug trial that poisons Byeong-gu's mother is depicted as typical of corporate behavior prioritizing profit over human lives. The film portrays South Korea's chaebol system—large family-owned conglomerates—as fundamentally predatory, using their economic power to escape consequences for harm caused to ordinary citizens.

**Alienation and Isolation:** Both Byeong-gu and Su-ni are profoundly isolated figures. Byeong-gu's obsession with conspiracy theories has alienated him from normal society, while Su-ni's childlike appearance and circus performer status mark her as an outsider. Their relationship, while caring, is also codependent and disconnected from reality. The film suggests that conspiracy thinking can be a form of existential coping mechanism for those who feel powerless in a system rigged against them.

**Genre Deconstruction:** By blending black comedy, psychological thriller, and science fiction, the film subverts audience expectations about each genre. The "alien invasion" narrative turns out to be real, but in an inverted way—the "alien" is a human collaborator with extraterrestrials who destroys Earth not through invasion but by declaring humanity a failed experiment. This twist inverts typical science fiction narratives where humanity either unites against alien threats or is saved by contact with a superior civilization.

**Class and Power:** The film's power dynamic between the wealthy pharmaceutical executive Kang and the working-class Byeong-gu reflects class tensions in South Korean society. Kang possesses the economic power to commit crimes with impunity, while Byeong-gu has no recourse through legitimate channels when his mother is harmed. The conspiracy theory becomes a way for someone without power to construct a narrative where they matter, where their actions have cosmic significance.

Save the Green Planet! remains significant as a cult classic that predicted the contemporary moment where distrust of institutions has become mainstream. Its prescient themes of corporate power, government secrecy, and the psychological toll of feeling powerless have only become more relevant in the decades since its release.
