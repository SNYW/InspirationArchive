# The New Barbarians (1983)

**Tags:** 🌍 Multinational | 🎭 Gritty | 📚 Post-Apocalypse | 🧬 Religion, Ecology

**Medium:** Film
**Raw Score:** 3.0/5
**Total Ratings:** 50

**Creator/Author:** Enzo G. Castellari (Director), Tito Carpi (Writer)

## The Pitch

In the year 2019, after nuclear war has devastated Earth, a ruthless gang called "The Templars" roams the wasteland, systematically exterminating survivors in a twisted campaign of genocide. When a former Templar known as Scorpion encounters a small group of religious colonists being hunted, he must choose between his violent past and protecting the innocent. This Italian cult classic delivers gritty post-apocalyptic action while exploring themes of redemption, faith, and the darkness that lurks within humanity.

## The Truth

The world has been destroyed by nuclear war in 2019. Humanity has been reduced to scattered groups of starving survivors. A vicious gang called "The Templars" has emerged, led by the fanatical One, who believes that humanity must be purged to allow the Earth to heal. The Templars travel the wasteland on motorcycles and in armed vehicles, attacking settlements and slaughtering anyone they encounter.

Scorpion (Giancarlo Prete), a former Templar who has grown disillusioned with their violent ways, roams the wasteland alone. After witnessing the Templars murder a family, he encounters Nadir (Fred Williamson), a tough loner with his own code of survival. Together, they come across a small group of religious colonists led by Father Moses, traveling to find a new home.

When the Templars pursue the colonists, Scorpion and Nadir decide to protect them. They lead the colonists through dangerous territory while being hunted by the Templars. During the journey, Scorpion must confront his past and his former brothers-in-arms. The Templars catch up to them in an abandoned city, leading to an epic confrontation. In the final battle, Scorpion faces off against his former comrade Shadow, ultimately defeating the Templar leadership and allowing the colonists to escape to safety.

The film ends with Scorpion and Nadir deciding to continue wandering the wasteland, but now as protectors of the innocent rather than survivors alone.

## The Core

"The New Barbarians" is a quintessential example of the Italian post-apocalyptic film genre that emerged in the early 1980s. Director Enzo G. Castellari creates a grim vision of a future where civilization has collapsed and humanity has reverted to tribal violence. The film can be seen as a commentary on the futility of violence and the possibility of redemption.

The Templars represent a particularly dark interpretation of ideological extremism - they believe they are serving a higher purpose by eliminating humanity, mirroring real-world examples of genocidal ideologies. Scorpion's journey from Templar to protector represents the possibility of moral redemption even for those who have committed terrible acts.

Visually, the film is notable for its use of the Italian countryside to create the post-apocalyptic wasteland, with practical effects and stunt work that defined the genre. The film's score by Claudio Simonetti adds to the atmospheric, gritty tone.

The film also explores themes of faith versus survival, as the religious colonists represent hope and humanity's better nature, while the Templars represent the complete surrender to despair and violence. This tension provides the thematic backbone for the action sequences and character development.
