# When It Changed (1972)

**Tags:** 🌍 USA | 🎭 Optimistic | 📚 Utopia | 🧬 Rebellion, Exploration

**Medium:** Short Story
**Raw Score:** 4.0/5
**Total Ratings:** 500

**Creator/Author:** Joanna Russ

## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)
On the far-off planet Whileaway, a plague wiped out men centuries ago, leaving women to build a peaceful, rational society free from the hierarchies and violence of the old world. When a ship from Earth finally arrives—carrying men who want to "resettle" the planet and reclaim it for humanity—the women of Whileaway face an existential choice. This Nebula Award-winning short story is a sharp, subversive examination of gender, power, and what "civilization" truly means. Joanna Russ writes with incisive wit and fierce intelligence, turning the conventions of space colonization science fiction inside out.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

The story is told through the perspective of Janet, a woman living on Whileaway who works as a "computer woman"—essentially maintaining the planetary networks that keep their society functioning. The society is small (only 3,000 people), isolated, and has developed over centuries without men. The women of Whileaway have created a culture that values reason, equality, and cooperation. They have eliminated many of the constructs Earth still holds dear: competitive hierarchies, property disputes, and gender-based dynamics power.

When the spaceship arrives from Earth, carrying a team of "Founders" meant to re-establish human civilization on Whileaway, the equilibrium shatters. The Founders include both women and men—the first men the Whileawayans have ever seen. The Whileawayans are initially curious and welcoming, but tensions quickly emerge.

The Earth women attempt to explain Whileaway's social structure to the newcomers, but the Earth men are disturbed by what they see as a "broken" society—one without men, without marriage, without the gender roles they consider natural. The men begin attempting to restructure Whileaway according to their understanding of "proper" human society, asserting dominance and attempting to establish hierarchical relationships.

The climax comes when the men attempt to assert control over the Whileawayans, essentially trying to "colonize" the women and remake their society in Earth's image. The women must decide whether to resist, and if so, how—and whether their carefully constructed peaceful society can survive contact with the patriarchal structures the men bring with them.

The story ends ambiguously but powerfully, suggesting that the encounter has fundamentally changed both the Whileawayans and the Earth newcomers, whether for better or worse remaining open to interpretation.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)
"When It Changed" is fundamentally about the social construction of gender and power. Russ critiques the idea that patriarchal structures are "natural" or inevitable, instead presenting them as historical constructs that can be—and have been—eliminated. The Whileawayans represent a thought experiment: what would human society look like without men? Russ's answer is striking in its ordinariness—without gender hierarchy, the women simply get on with living, working, and caring for each other.

The story also examines colonization and cultural imperialism. The Earth men don't see the Whileawayans as equals; they see a "lost" society that needs to be "saved" and reformed. This mirror's real-world colonial attitudes, particularly regarding gender. Russ is pointing out that the "civilizing" mission of colonialism is often about imposing one's own structures rather than genuine concern for others.

Additionally, the story explores what "civilization" truly means. The Earth view equates civilization with gender roles, marriage, and hierarchy—structures the Whileawayans have rejected. The Whileawayans are not primitive; they have advanced technology, art, and philosophy. Their society is simply organized differently. Russ asks whether a peaceful, equal society can truly be called "uncivilized."

The title itself is significant: "When It Changed" refers to the moment of contact, when the Whileawayans' isolated existence ended and they were forced to confront the wider galaxy. It's about what happens when different worldviews collide—and who gets to define what comes after.
