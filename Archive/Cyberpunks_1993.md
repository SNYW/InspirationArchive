# Cyberpunks (1993)

## The Pitch

Cyberpunks is a top-down shooter developed by Mutation and published by Core Design in 1993 exclusively for the Amiga computer. The game places players in control of three characters simultaneously, tasking them with navigating hostile alien environments, shooting enemy forces, and locating door passes to progress through levels. Released during the height of the cyberpunk aesthetic's popularity in media, Cyberpunks represents a fascinating period piece of early 1990s British game development, combining the twin-stick shooter mechanics popular at the time with themes of corporate warfare and artificial intelligence. The game was developed by the relatively obscure British studio Mutation, with Adrian Robert Cummings handling design, art, and music—a notable feat of single-developer ambition for the era.

## The Truth

The player controls a squad of three cyberpunk operatives who must fight their way through alien-infested levels. Gameplay involves simultaneous control of all three characters, each with their own abilities and loadouts. The objective in each level is to eliminate hostile alien forces and locate door passes—key cards or access codes required to advance to subsequent areas. The game features multiple levels with increasing difficulty, requiring players to manage all three characters' positions and actions in real-time.

The narrative context, while minimal, establishes a world where megacorporations have developed advanced cybernetic soldiers to combat alien threats. The player characters are enhanced operatives—cyborg warriors with superior reflexes and firepower tasked with missions in hostile territories. The enemy forces are alien in origin, requiring the players to adapt to different combat scenarios as they progress. The game emphasizes action over story, with the plot serving primarily as a framework for the shooting mechanics rather than a deep narrative experience.

## The Core

**Corporate Warfare and Cybernetic Enhancement:** The game reflects early 1990s anxieties about the intersection of corporate power and military technology. The megacorporations depicted as sending cyborg soldiers on dangerous missions embody concerns about private military forces operating beyond governmental oversight. The enhancement of human soldiers with cybernetic implants positions the game within the broader cyberpunk theme of human-machine fusion and the blurring of boundaries between organic and artificial.

**Simultaneous Multi-Character Control:** The game's most distinctive mechanical feature—controlling three characters at once—represents both a technical achievement for the Amiga platform and a design philosophy that rewards strategic multitasking. Players must coordinate all three operatives, positioning them optimally and managing their individual health and ammunition resources. This mechanic was relatively rare in 1990s shooters and demonstrates Mutation's ambition to create something beyond a typical run-and-gun experience.

**Amiga Era Preservation:** Cyberpunks exists as a time capsule of mid-1990s British gaming culture. The Amiga platform was particularly popular in Europe, especially in the UK, and Core Design was a prominent publisher known for titles like the early Tomb Raider games. The collaboration between the small studio Mutation and established publisher Core Design represents a moment in gaming history before the consolidation of the industry into fewer, larger publishers. The game's existence documents the diversity of creative voices active in that era.
