# La Jetée (1962)

**Tags:** 🌍 France | 🎭 Melancholic | 📚 Post-Apocalypse | 🧬 Memory, Time-Travel

**Medium:** Short Film / Featurette
**Raw Score:** 4.1/5
**Total Ratings:** 40000
**Creator/Author:** Chris Marker

## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)
La Jetée is a 28-minute French sci-fi featurette constructed almost entirely from still photographs, telling the haunting tale of a post-nuclear time travel experiment. In the aftermath of World War III, survivors live underground in Paris while scientists attempt time travel to save humanity. The protagonist, haunted by a childhood memory of a woman at Orly Airport, becomes their test subject. This visually stunning and philosophically rich film explores memory, time, fate, and the impossibility of escape from one's own timeline. Though made with minimal resources—a few seconds of motion picture footage and hundreds of still photographs—it achieves a hypnotic, dreamlike quality that has influenced countless filmmakers, most notably Terry Gilliam (12 Monkeys).

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)
In post-apocalyptic Paris, survivors inhabit the underground galleries of the Palais de Chaillot after World War III has devastated the surface. Scientists working on time travel experiments seek subjects who can mentally withstand the traumatic experience of temporal displacement. They eventually choose a man whose childhood memory of a woman at Orly Airport's observation platform has become an obsessive fixation.

The protagonist successfully travels to the pre-war period, where he encounters the woman from his memory. They develop a romantic relationship during his repeated journeys to the past. The experiment's success leads the scientists to attempt sending him far into the future, where technologically advanced beings provide him with a power unit sufficient to rebuild civilization.

However, upon returning to the present, the protagonist learns he is to be executed by his jailers. The future people offer to take him permanently to their time, but he instead requests to be returned to his childhood at the airport. Arriving at the jetty, he spots the woman—and notices an agent of his jailers has followed him. In his final moments, he realizes the incident he witnessed as a child (a man dying) was his own death, completing the stable time loop.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)
La Jetée is a profound meditation on memory, time, and determinism. Chris Marker constructs the entire film from still photographs, creating a unique cinematic language where the gap between images becomes the space of imagination—a formal choice that mirrors the film's thematic concern with the nature of perception and memory.

The film explores whether we can ever truly escape our fate or whether time is a closed loop where past, present, and future are predetermined. The protagonist's obsessive return to a specific memory suggests that we are trapped by our own psyches, replaying past moments rather than creating new futures.

The use of still photographs also comments on the nature of historical memory—how we construct narrative from frozen moments, how the past is always already gone yet perpetually present in our minds. The film suggests that both memory and history are constructions, subject to manipulation and repetition.

La Jetée influenced virtually every subsequent time travel narrative in cinema, particularly in its fatalistic view of time and its exploration of the psychological toll of temporal displacement. It remains a foundational text of the French Left Bank movement and a cornerstone of experimental science fiction cinema.
