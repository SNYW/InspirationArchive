# The Day the Sky Exploded (1958)

**Tags:** 🌍 Italy | 🎭 Pessimistic | 📚 Hard-SF, Dystopia | 🧬 Exploration, Ecology, Religion

**Medium:** Film
**Raw Score:** 2.25/5
**Total Ratings:** 200

**Creator/Author:** Directed by Paolo Heusch and Mario Bava

## The Pitch

In 1958, the first Italian science fiction film was born—and it's a paranoid, apocalyptic masterpiece. When the United Nations launches an atomic rocket toward the Moon, a malfunction causes the booster to explode in the Delta asteroid cluster, dislodging asteroids from their orbits. They merge into one giant cluster heading for Earth, threatening humanity with extinction. What follows is a visually stunning race against time, featuring groundbreaking special effects by a young Mario Bava, who would later become a legend of Italian horror cinema. This is Cold War anxiety crystallized into 82 minutes of dread, disaster, and desperate hope.

## The Truth

From Cape Shark in the Australian desert, the United Nations launches an atomic rocket on a crewed Moon mission, but one of the engines malfunctions. American pilot John MacLaren disengages the escape capsule and returns to Earth safely, but the atomic booster continues into space and explodes in the Delta asteroid cluster. 

When MacLaren insists on staying at Cape Shark to help with the situation, his homesick wife Mary takes their son and leaves for the U.S. without him. Meanwhile, the rocket explosion dislodges the asteroids from their orbits. They merge into one giant cluster heading for Earth. Scientists discover the cluster will pass the Moon, causing tidal effects which will flood coastal regions.

As the cluster approaches, it causes worldwide disasters: tidal waves, wind, firestorms, and earthquakes. Mass evacuations lead to panic and riots. Engineer Peter Leduq and his colleague Katie Dandridge work frantically to calculate the results of the rocket's explosion while their personal drama unfolds—their relationship evolves from a bet-driven flirtation to genuine connection under the pressure of impending doom.

At the eleventh hour, MacLaren comes up with the idea of arming every missile on Earth with a nuclear warhead and firing them all at the cluster. Due to the climate change wrought by the cluster, Cape Shark's air conditioning must remain at maximum power to keep the temperature low enough for the base's calculator to determine all the firing data.

Scientist Dr. Randowsky becomes convinced that the chaos caused by the cluster is divine punishment for humanity's use of nuclear missiles. He disables the air conditioning unit, rendering the calculator inoperative, and uses a gun to hold off anyone who approaches to restore it. MacLaren, Leduq, and MacLaren's friend Herbert Weisser confront Randowsky, and Weisser is fatally shot. Randowsky runs out of ammunition and accidentally electrocutes himself while fleeing.

The calculator is restarted, and the last of the firing data is transmitted. The missiles are launched, destroying the cluster and saving Earth. MacLaren is reunited with his family just in time.

## The Core

This film is a fascinating artifact of its era—a Cold War parable wrapped in the trappings of 1950s sci-fi. The collaboration between Paolo Heusch and Mario Bava (who served as cinematographer and is rumored to have done most of the actual directing) created something unique in the sci-fi canon: an Italian perspective on the American-dominated genre.

The film uses extensive stock footage—which gives it an almost documentary quality as real-world disaster footage intercuts with the narrative. Bava's special effects, though primitive by later standards, are inventive, particularly the image of the asteroid cluster looming over cityscapes.

Thematically, the film explores humanity's hubris in tampering with atomic power, the divine punishment narrative offered by Dr. Randowsky, and ultimately the triumph of human ingenuity over cosmic catastrophe. It's a quintessential "disaster from space" film that predates similar Hollywood productions and offers a uniquely European (and specifically Italian) take on apocalyptic science fiction.
