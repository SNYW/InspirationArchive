# THX 1138 (1971)

## The Pitch

In a dystopian future deep underground, humanity lives in a sterile, drug-regulated society where emotions are chemically suppressed and android police enforce strict conformity. Citizens are identified by alphanumeric designations rather than names, stripped of individuality and humanity. When worker THX 1138 begins experiencing strange side effects from his medication, he discovers feelings he never knew existed—a dangerous journey that leads to rebellion, escape, and a haunting final confrontation with the system that enslaved him. Visually stunning and atmospherically dense, this is George Lucas's feature debut, made years before Star Wars transformed cinema.

## The Truth

THX 1138 is set in a future underground society where sexual intercourse and reproduction are prohibited, and mandatory mind-altering drugs ensure compliance. Workers wear identical white uniforms with shaven heads to emphasize uniformity, while police androids dress in black. Instead of names, citizens have designations with three letters and four digits.

THX 1138 works in a factory producing android police officers. His roommate LUH secretly substitutes his sedatives with placebos, causing him to develop emotions, nausea, and sexual desires. THX and LUH fall in love and have sex—acts that are criminal in this society.

THX is eventually arrested and undergoes drug therapy. At his trial, it's revealed he was clinically "born." Rather than termination, he's sentenced to prison alongside SEN. During their escape, they're joined by hologram actor SRT 5752. THX learns that LUH has been "consumed" and her name reassigned to her fetus in a growth chamber.

During the chase, THX escapes to the city limits. Android officers are ordered to cease pursuit when the cost of capture exceeds their budget. THX continues upward and discovers the city is entirely underground—he escapes to the surface and witnesses his first sunset.

## The Core

THX 1138 is a powerful meditation on conformity, surveillance, and the human spirit's resistance to oppression. Lucas presents a society where individuality has been chemically and systematically eliminated, using the alphanumeric designations to show how bureaucracy reduces people to numbers. The mandatory drug use represents pharmaceutical control—a precursor to later anxieties about mood-altering medications. The android police represent technological oppression, while the underground setting symbolizes how totalitarianism traps its citizens in manufactured reality.

The film also explores themes of rebirth and escape. THX's journey from compliant worker to rebel mirrors a spiritual awakening. His final emergence into sunlight represents not just physical escape but psychological liberation—the birth (or rebirth) into authentic human experience. The fetus subplot raises questions about reproduction, ownership, and whether children born into such systems can ever truly be free.
