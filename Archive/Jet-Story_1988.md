# Jet-Story (1988)

## The Pitch

Jet-Story is a 1988 action game developed by Cybexlab Software for the ZX Spectrum, published by Ultrasoft. The player controls a spacecraft navigating through a massive maze-like world containing 128 distinct areas. The objective is to locate and destroy 47 enemy bases scattered throughout this labyrinthine environment. The game is notable for its ambitious scope—a vast interconnected world filled with hostile forces in an era when ZX Spectrum titles typically featured more confined environments. Developed primarily by Miroslav Fídler with musical contributions from František Fuka, Jet-Story represents a distinctive Czechoslovakian contribution to the ZX Spectrum library.

## The Truth

The player assumes control of a spacecraft operating within an enormous maze structure spanning 128 unique areas. The primary mission involves locating and destroying all 47 enemy bases distributed throughout this world. The gameplay demands strategic navigation as players must chart paths through the maze while engaging hostile forces.

The challenge is substantial: the maze contains numerous enemies that patrol its corridors and attack on sight. Players have access to weapons but face a constrained ammunition supply, adding a resource management layer to the exploration and combat. Throughout the game world, players can collect supply boxes containing essential resources: ammunition for continuing combat, fuel for the spacecraft, and health pickups to repair damage. The physics simulation includes realistic gravity effects, unusual for ZX Spectrum titles of this period.

The game received positive reception upon release, with Bit Magazine awarding it 84%. Critics praised the gameplay depth and the soundtrack composed by František Fuka, though the simplified graphics drew some criticism. Originally released in 1988, the rights were acquired by Ultrasoft in 1992, leading to a re-release. The title serves as a sequel to the earlier Cybexlab title Planet of Shades, expanding on concepts established in that game.

## The Core

Jet-Story exemplifies the creative output from Czechoslovakia's early video game industry during the late 1980s. At a time when home computer development in communist-era Czechoslovakia was constrained by limited access to Western markets and technology, developers like Miroslav Fídler created ambitious projects that pushed the hardware's capabilities. The game's enormous world size—128 areas—demonstrates the ambition of Czech programmers working within the limitations of 8-bit hardware.

The combination of maze exploration, combat, and resource management creates a challenging experience that rewards methodical play. The realistic gravity simulation adds a unique physics element not commonly found in contemporary titles. The game's Czech origins also represent a piece of cultural history—software development in Czechoslovakia before the Velvet Revolution of 1989 operated under significantly different conditions than in the West, making achievements like Jet-Story particularly notable.
