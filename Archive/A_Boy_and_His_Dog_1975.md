# A Boy and His Dog (1975)

**Medium:** Film

**Creator/Author:** L.Q. Jones (Director), Based on novella by Harlan Ellison

## The Pitch

In a post-nuclear America of 2024, an 18-year-old scavenger named Vic roams the Southwestern wasteland with his telepathic dog, Blood. Vic cares only about food and sex; Blood is a well-read, misanthropic genius who can read minds and locate survivors. When they discover an underground society called Topeka—run by a committee of white-faced officials who use genetic engineering and artificial insemination to reproduce—their contrasting desires trap them between survival on the surface and the twisted utopia below.

## The Truth

Vic and Blood survive in the post-apocalyptic wasteland by stealing from other survivors and evading raiders, mutants, and berserk military androids. Their relationship is transactional: Blood helps Vic find women to rape in exchange for food, since the genetically-engineered dog cannot forage for himself.

During a raid on an abandoned bunker, they encounter Quilla June Holmes, a seductive young woman from Downunder—an underground civilization with an artificial biosphere hidden beneath the ruins of Topeka, Kansas. Downunder is ruled by a committee that has created a bizarre replica of pre-war America, with all citizens wearing whiteface and 1930s-style clothing.

Quilla June tricks Vic into following her underground, promising him endless women. Instead, he learns he has been brought to Topeka as a "stud"—his genetic material will be extracted via electroejaculation to impregnate the female population, after which he will be killed on "the farm." When Quilla June orchestrates a rebellion against the Committee's android enforcer Michael, she fails and is also sentenced to the farm.

On the surface, Vic finds Blood starving. Quilla June demands Vic abandon the dog, but Vic chooses Blood. The film's final act reveals Vic has killed Quilla June and fed her body to Blood to keep the dog alive. The pair walk away into the wasteland, with Blood joking about her "poor judgment" in choosing men.

## The Core

A Boy and His Dog is a pitch-black comedy that explores humanity's descent into brutal survivalism. The film questions whether civilization's collapse strips away not just social structures but fundamental morality—Vic has no ethical framework, only appetites. Blood represents education and civilization's voice, but he's complicit in Vic's atrocities.

The underground society of Topeka serves as a dark mirror: it preserved technology and created a "utopia," but at the cost of individual freedom and humanity. The Committee's bizarre simulation of pre-war America comments on how societies reconstruct themselves after catastrophe—often replicating the worst aspects of what came before.

The relationship between Vic and Blood is the film's heart: an uneasy alliance between human instinct and canine intellect. The ambiguous ending—where moral lines have been crossed beyond redemption—leaves viewers questioning whether any of humanity is worth saving.
