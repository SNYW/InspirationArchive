## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)
Donna is an ugly woman who works in an office where she's completely ignored by her colleagues. Her life changes when she discovers "the neural" - a neuralinterface system that allows people to experience virtual reality through perfect physical avatars called "dummies." Through the dummy, Donna becomes Trixie, a beautiful woman who experiences all the attention and desirability she was denied in her real body. But as her dual existence deepens, the line between reality and simulation begins to blur in terrifying ways.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)
Donna is a plain woman whose homely face has made her virtually invisible in her office environment. Her coworkers ignore her completely, speaking over her as if she doesn't exist. One day, she discovers a neural implant system that allows users to project their consciousness into perfect physical dummies - beautiful bodies that exist in a shared virtual space called "the neural."

Donna creates an account and takes on the persona of Trixie, a stunning blonde mannequin. For the first time in her life, she experiences what it's like to be beautiful, desired, and seen. Through Trixie, she meets Chester, a handsome young man who falls in love with her virtual persona. Their relationship develops entirely in the neural, and Chester believes he's found the perfect woman.

However, the story takes a dark turn when Chester decides he wants to meet Trixie in real life. Donna must confront the impossible choice between maintaining her perfect virtual existence and revealing her true physical self. The story ends ambiguously - it's unclear whether Donna can ever bridge the gap between her real and virtual identities, or whether she's doomed to live permanently in the space between.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)
This story is a pioneering work of feminist cyberpunk that explores several interconnected themes. First and foremost is the examination of how physical appearance - particularly for women - determines social value and visibility. Donna's complete erasure in her real life versus the attention she receives as Trixie creates a devastating critique of superficial beauty standards.

The story also explores questions of identity and authenticity in a digital age. When Donna can become someone else entirely in virtual space, which version of her is "real"? The dummy represents both liberation (from the prison of an unappealing body) and entrapment (a false existence dependent on technology).
