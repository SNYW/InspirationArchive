# Time to Hunt (2020)

**Tags:** 🌍 South-Korea | 🎭 Tense | 📚 Dystopia, Post-Apocalypse | 🧬 Rebellion, Megacorps

**Medium:** Film
**Raw Score:** 3.15/5
**Total Ratings:** 7700
**Creator/Author:** Yoon Sung-hyun (Director)

## The Pitch

Time to Hunt is a 2020 South Korean dystopian action thriller film directed by Yoon Sung-hyun. Set in a bleak future South Korea, the film follows a group of four friends living in an economically devastated society where unemployment is endemic and hope is scarce. With their backs against the wall, they devise a seemingly straightforward heist to steal money and start fresh. However, upon completing the job, they discover they have attracted the attention of a mysterious and relentless assassin who begins picking them off one by one. Released globally on Netflix during the early COVID-19 pandemic, the film garnered attention for its visceral action sequences and grim view of a near-future society where the gap between rich and poor has become untenable.

## The Truth

The film opens in a dystopian South Korea where economic collapse has left millions unemployed and desperate. Our four protagonists—Jun-hee, Sang-hoon, Jin-woo, and Woo-myung—scrape by in the lower echelons of society. Facing insurmountable debt and with no legitimate prospects, they decide to pull off a daring heist.

The heist itself goes smoothly. The group successfully steals a significant sum of money from an undisclosed target, and they part ways with plans to reunite and escape to a better life. But almost immediately, things begin to unravel. One by one, the friends are hunted down by a faceless, efficient assassin who seems to know their every move.

As the body count rises, the remaining members of the group are forced into increasingly desperate circumstances. The assassin—revealed to be part of a larger system of control—treats the hunt almost as sport, demonstrating the complete devaluation of human life in this dystopic world. The film culminates in a final confrontation that leaves the audience questioning whether survival in such a system is truly possible, or whether the system itself is designed to ensure that people like our protagonists never truly escape their circumstances.

## The Core

**Economic Inequality and Class Warfare:** Time to Hunt presents a future where the wealth gap has become absolute. The protagonists are not criminals by nature but by circumstance—driven to desperation by a system that offers them no legitimate way to survive. The film critiques late capitalism by showing how economic policies create a permanent underclass with no way out.

**The Surveillance State:** The assassin's seemingly supernatural ability to track the protagonists suggests a comprehensive surveillance apparatus. In this world, privacy is nonexistent, and escape is impossible. The state—or the megacorporations that control it—watches everything.

**Desperation as Narrative Engine:** Unlike traditional heist films where the crime is the focus, Time to Hunt uses the heist merely as a trigger. The real story is about how desperate people are exploited by a system that was never designed to help them. The "hunt" of the title takes on double meaning—not just a literal hunt by the assassin, but the way society hunts the vulnerable.

**Action as Social Commentary:** The film's visceral, brutal action sequences serve a thematic purpose. The violence is not glamorous but exhausting and nihilistic. Each death represents another casualty of a system that treats human life as expendable. The film's grim tone and ambiguous ending suggest that in such a world, there are no winners—only survivors and victims.
