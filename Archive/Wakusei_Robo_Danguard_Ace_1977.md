## The Pitch

Wakusei Robo Danguard Ace (Planetary Robot Danguard Ace) is a 56-episode Japanese anime television series created by the legendary Leiji Matsumoto—best known for Space Battleship Yamato and Galaxy Express 999—with Dan Kobayashi. Produced by Toei Animation and airing from March 1977 to March 1978, it was Matsumoto's sole venture into the mecha genre.

The story is set on a future Earth where natural resources have been depleted and humanity looks to the tenth planet, Promete, for the first exploration team is destroyed survival. When by betrayal, a megalomaniac named Mr. Doppler emerges, building a vast military force to claim Promete for himself. Earth's governments begin constructing massive combat robots to oppose him, but Doppler destroys them all—except for Danguard Ace. The series follows Takuma Ichimonji, son of the man who led the doomed Promete expedition, who joins Danguard's base as a cadet hoping to restore his father's honor.

## The Truth

The series opens with humanity's desperation as Earth's resources dwindle, driving nations to attempt colonization of the distant planet Promete. The initial mission ends in tragedy when Takuma's father and his team are betrayed and destroyed. This event sets in motion the emergence of Mr. Doppler, a former associate who seizes control of the Promete situation, declaring that only he may approach the planet.

Earth's governments respond by commissioning massive combat robots, but Doppler annihilates each one before completion. Only Danguard Ace survives, housed in a secret base and piloted by the mysterious Captain Dan—a man who previously served Doppler under mind control. Takuma Ichimonji arrives at the Danguard base as a cadet, driven by the desire to prove his father's loyalty and fight against Doppler's tyranny.

The series follows the ongoing conflict between Danguard Ace and Doppler's forces, featuring numerous battles as Earth's forces attempt to reach Promete. The character dynamics center on Takuma's growth as a soldier and his quest to uncover the truth about his father's betrayal. The show incorporates typical 1970s mecha elements while featuring distinctive character designs by Matsumoto that would influence later Space Battleship Yamato productions.

Danguard Ace also spawned two theatrical films: "Danguard Ace vs. Insect Robot Troop" (July 1977) and "Danguard Ace: The Great Space War" (March 1978).

## The Core

**Leiji Matsumoto's Mecha Experiment:** Danguard Ace represents a unique entry in Matsumoto's oeuvre as his only mecha anime. While sharing visual and tonal elements with his more famous works—space opera aesthetics, heroic characters, themes of exploration and sacrifice—the series also demonstrates his approach to the robot genre. The show features his characteristic blend of dramatic storytelling with themes of environmental desperation and corporate/individual greed.

**Resource Depletion and Space Colonization:** The series reflects 1970s anxieties about resource scarcity, projecting Earth's depletion onto a future where humanity must look beyond the solar system for survival. This premise positions the conflict not as simple good versus evil but as a struggle over humanity's future, with Doppler representing the hoarding of resources and potential.

**Father-Son Narrative and Legacy:** Takuma Ichimonji's storyline centers on honor, legacy, and redemption. His father's perceived betrayal casts a shadow over his identity, driving his choice to fight. This personal quest for truth and restoration provides emotional stakes beyond the military conflict.

**International Legacy:** Despite its relatively obscure status compared to Matsumoto's other works, Danguard Ace influenced Western audiences through various English-language releases, including the "Force Five" package in the U.S. The titular robot also appeared in Marvel Comics' Shogun Warriors line, introducing the character to American audiences in the late 1970s.
