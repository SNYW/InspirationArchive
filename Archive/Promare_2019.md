## The Pitch

Promare is a 2019 Japanese animated science fiction action film directed by Hiroyuki Imaishi and produced by Trigger. Set in a world ravaged by the "Great World Blaze" thirty years prior—where spontaneous human combustion killed half the population and left some survivors with pyrokinetic abilities known as the "Burnish"—the film follows Galo Thymos, a newly recruited member of Burning Rescue, a firefighting organization in the futuristic city of Promepolis. When Galo confronts the Mad Burnish, a group of radical pyrokinetic terrorists, he encounters Lio Fotia, their leader, and uncovers a vast conspiracy involving the city's governor Kray Foresight, who plans to use the Burnish's powers for human experimentation and to flee Earth as the planet faces destruction from within its core.

## The Truth

The film opens with the aftermath of the Great World Blaze, a catastrophic event that caused mass spontaneous human combustion and killed half the world's population. Those who survived developed pyrokinetic abilities and became known as the Burnish. Thirty years later, the world has rebuilt around the city of Promepolis, where Galo Thymos serves in Burning Rescue, a firefighting group that responds to incidents involving the Mad Burnish.

During a mission, Galo defeats the Mad Burnish leader Lio Fotia, but Lio reveals that Governor Kray Foresight has been capturing Burnish for human experimentation. Shocked—since Galo idolizes Kray, who saved him from a fire as a child—he confronts the Governor, only to be arrested for treason. Meanwhile, Kray's police force Freeze Force recaptures most of the Burnish population. Lio escapes but transforms into a giant flaming dragon in a rage, burning down part of the city.

Galo escapes prison and, with help from Burning Rescue, confronts Lio at a frozen lake. There, they discover a hidden laboratory run by a holographic AI of Deus Prometh, a scientist Kray killed. Deus reveals that the Burnish are connected to the Promare, interdimensional flame beings who came to Earth when a dimensional rift opened. The planet's magma surge is a side effect of the Prometh's pain from Kray's experiments, and his warp drive plan would destroy Earth.

In the climax, Galo and Lio combine forces in a mecha called Deus X Machina to confront Kray. After defeating him and learning the truth—that Kray himself started the fire that led to his political career—they save Lio and the world. The Promare envelop the surface, allowing them to burn harmlessly and closing the rift. The Burnish become normal humans, and Galo and Lio resolve to rebuild the world together.

## The Core

**Pyrokinetic as Metaphor for Difference:** The Burnish represent marginalized groups whose abilities mark them as different and dangerous to society. Their persecution and Kray's exploitation of them mirrors historical treatments of minorities and outcasts, exploring themes of otherness, discrimination, and the political weaponization of fear.

The film critiques hero worship and blind obedience to power, showing how those in power manipulate narratives and use crises to consolidate control. Galo's journey represents waking up to systemic injustice.

**Hope and Rebuilding:** Despite its dystopian premise, Promare is fundamentally optimistic. The resolution shows that systemic change requires challenging authority and working together across divides. The Burnish's sacrifice to close the rift and become ordinary humans suggests that transcending difference is possible through collective action.

**Trigger's Signature Style:** The film showcases Trigger's explosive animation style—flamboyant action sequences, vibrant colors, and exaggerated character designs. The mecha designs and action choreography harken back to classic anime while the visual approach is distinctly modern. The film's aesthetic embodies its themes of passion and burning spirit.

Promare received critical acclaim for its visual spectacle, with a 97% rating on Rotten Tomatoes. It was nominated for Best Animated Feature at the Annie Awards and won the Newtype Anime Award for Best Theatrical Screening. The film grossed over ¥1.5 billion in Japan, making it one of Trigger's most successful original projects.
