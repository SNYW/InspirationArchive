## The Pitch

In 2032 Tokyo, seven years after a catastrophic earthquake split the city in two, an all-female mercenary group called the Knight Sabers fights rogue robots and corporate threats while wearing powered exoskeletons. The series explores a cyberpunk megacity dominated by the evil Genom corporation, whose synthetic cyborg "Boomers" have gone out of control. The Knight Sabers—Sylia, Priss, Linna, and Nene—operate outside the law, taking on missions the underfunded AD Police can't handle.

## The Truth

The OVA series spans eight episodes released between 1987 and 1991. Each episode tells a self-contained story while building toward larger arcs involving Genom's corruption and the Knight Sabers' mysterious origins.

In the first episode, "Knight Sabers," we see Sylia Stingray recruit the other to form three women the team after her father—the creator of the Knight Sabers' exoskeletons—is killed. Each woman has a personal stake: Priss is a street performer, Linna works at a nightclub, and Nene is a wealthy heiress rebelling against her family.

In later episodes, relationships develop between the Knight Sabers and members of the AD Police, particularly between Linna and detective Leon McNichol. The final episodes reveal deeper conspiracies about Genom's plans and the true nature of the Boomer technology.

The series ends with the Knight Sabers successfully defeating Genom's schemes, though at significant personal cost. The OVAs spawned multiple sequels, including "Bubblegum Crash" (1991) and the television series "Bubblegum Crisis Tokyo 2040" (1998).

## The Core

Bubblegum Crisis serves as a quintessential example of 1980s cyberpunk anime, heavily influenced by Blade Runner and Streets of Fire. The series explores themes of corporate power run amok, the blurring line between human and machine, and female empowerment in a male-dominated world.

The "Boomer" technology—a synthetic cyborg designed to serve humanity but turning deadly—reflects anxieties about automation and artificial intelligence that resonate even more today. Genom Corporation represents the danger of unchecked corporate power and technological advancement without ethical oversight.

The Knight Sabers themselves represent a unique take on vigilantes—women who reject traditional societal roles to become warriors operating in the shadows. Their powered exoskeletons ("hardsuits") were groundbreaking animation for the time and influenced countless subsequent mecha and powered suit designs in anime.

The series also touches on class divisions in post-earthquake Tokyo, where wealth disparities became even more pronounced after the disaster forced American annexation. This political subtext adds depth to what could have been a simple action series.
