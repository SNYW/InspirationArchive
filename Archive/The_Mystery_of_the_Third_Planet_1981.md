# The Mystery of the Third Planet (1981)

**Tags:** 🌍 Russia | 🎭 Optimistic | 📚 Space-Opera | 🧬 Exploration, Aliens

**Medium:** Animated Film

**Creator/Author:** Directed by Roman Kachanov; Based on "Alisa's Travel" by Kir Bulychev

## The Pitch

When Captain Zelyony, Professor Seleznyov, and his daughter Alisa set out from Earth aboard the starship Pegas to collect rare animals for the Moscow Zoo, they expect a routine expedition. Instead, they stumble into a web of interplanetary mystery involving space pirates, extinct talking birds, and a double identity conspiracy. This charming Soviet animated feature blends childlike wonder with genuine sci-fi adventure, featuring distinctive hand-drawn animation and a score by composer Aleksandr Zatsepin.

## The Truth

The crew of the Pegas departs Earth on a mission to find exotic animals for the Moscow Zoo. Their journey takes them first to the Moon, where Professor Seleznyov meets his old friend Gromozeka, an alien archaeologist. Gromozeka directs them to the Planet of Two Captains, a massive museum dedicated to the legendary space explorers Captain Kim and Captain Buran, who circumnavigated the universe aboard the ship Blue Seagull.

At the museum, the heroes meet Doctor Verkhovtsev, who behaves suspiciously—refusing to show them the Captains' diaries and later being spotted on other planets. The heroes continue their journey to planet Bluk, where they discover the Chatterbirds—extinct birds capable of mimicking human speech and flying through open space. They learn that Doctor Verkhovtsev was responsible for the species' extinction.

Through a series of clues, the heroes track the last surviving Chatterbird to the Medusa system. There, they also discover that robots on the planet Shelezyaka have been malfunctioning due to diamond dust mixed into their grease—also the work of Verkhovtsev. Using mysterious mirror-flowers that record events in reverse, Alisa uncovers the truth: there are TWO Verkhovtsevs—one a respected scientist, the other the space pirate Glot in disguise.

The climax reveals that Captain Kim was trapped on the third planet of the Medusa system by Glot, who sought the formula for absolute fuel. Captain Buran arrives to help, and with the heroes' assistance, the pirates are defeated. The real Verkhovtsev had suspected the heroes from the beginning because of Glot's interest in the Captains' diaries. In the end, the heroes return to Earth with the captured pirate and their collection of rare animals, while Funnyguy Oo escapes briefly but is carried away by a local bird of prey.

## The Core

The Mystery of the Third Planet represents a fascinating artifact from the Soviet Union's engagement with children's science fiction. The film explores themes of scientific curiosity, the value of discovery, and the moral complexity beneath seemingly trustworthy exteriors—its villain hides behind the mask of a respected academic. The film also presents an optimistic vision of space exploration as a collaborative, peaceful endeavor, with aliens and humans working together across planetary boundaries.

The animation style blends Soviet artistic traditions with the sense of wonder typical of 1970s-80s animated family films, creating something that feels both distinctly Eastern European and universally appealing. The film's influence extends beyond cinema—it spawned video games and a 2009 spiritual successor, and has achieved cult classic status in Russia. For Western audiences unfamiliar with Soviet animation, it offers a window into a rich but often overlooked tradition of sci-fi storytelling.
