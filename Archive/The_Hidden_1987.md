# The Hidden (1987)

**Medium:** Film

**Creator/Author:** Directed by Jack Sholder, Written by Jim Kouf

## The Pitch

When alien entities start possessing ordinary citizens in Los Angeles, turning them into violent killers, an FBI agent with a secret reveals he's actually an extraterrestrial lawman pursuing a rogue alien parasite that has escaped to Earth. The catch? The alien can jump from body to body, making it nearly impossible to destroy. What follows is a visceral cat-and-mouse chase through the streets of LA as the alien hops from host to host, leaving a trail of bodies—and the question of what it truly means to be human.

## The Truth

The film opens with Jack DeVries, a seemingly normal citizen, suddenly robbing a bank and killing security guards before leading police on a high-speed chase. After being shot, he's taken to a hospital where Detective Tom Beck questions him. Meanwhile, FBI Special Agent Lloyd Gallagher arrives, insisting DeVries must be found.

At the hospital, DeVries dies—but not before a slug-like alien emerges from his mouth and transfers into the body of another comatose patient, Jonathan Miller. Miller then goes on a violent rampage, killing a record store owner, stealing a Ferrari, and sexually murdering a stripper before the alien jumps again.

Gallagher reveals to Beck that he's actually an extraterrestrial being pursuing an alien "thrill killer" that murdered his family and partner on another planet. The alien continues its rampage, taking over bodies of a stripper, a police lieutenant's dog, the lieutenant himself, Detective Willis, and finally Senator Holt—a presidential candidate.

Throughout the film, Gallagher demonstrates his alien nature: he's immune to bullets, wields advanced weapons, and eventually reveals that the real Lloyd Gallagher died in a fire, which he impersonated. The climax occurs at a press conference where Gallagher torchs Senator Holt's body to force the alien out, then kills it with his alien weapon.

In the end, as Beck lies dying, Gallagher transfers his life force into Beck, essentially becoming human to save his partner.

## The Core

The Hidden is a meditation on identity and humanity wrapped in sci-fi action. The alien parasite represents pure id—it takes over bodies and satisfies immediate urges without morality or conscience. Meanwhile, Gallagher, despite being an alien, demonstrates compassion, sacrifice, and loyalty—becoming more "human" than the humans he protects.

The film explores the question of what makes us human: Is it our bodies, or our choices? Gallagher gives up his immortality to save Beck, suggesting that love and sacrifice define humanity more than biology. The body-hopping also raises questions about identity—if your mind and personality are replaced by an alien, are you still you?

The film also functions as a critique of American violence—ordinary citizens become killers, and the alien specifically targets positions of power (senators, police) to gain influence.
