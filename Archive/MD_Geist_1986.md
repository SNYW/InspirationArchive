# M.D. Geist (1986)

## The Pitch

M.D. Geist (aka Sōkihei M.D. Geist, "Armoured Demon Soldier M.D. Geist") is a gritty 1986 OVA set on the war-torn planet Jerra. The story follows Geist, a genetically-engineered supersoldier created using "Bioclone theory" - designed to be the ultimate killing machine. Due to his overwhelming ferocity in battle, he was placed in suspended animation and launched into orbit, only to crash back down years later. Now, with the world changed and both the Regular Army and the rebellious Negsrom Army engaged in a doomsday conflict, Geist must stop the "Death Force" - a weapon designed to annihilate all life on the planet. Known for its extreme violence, dark tone, and influence on later cyberpunk and post-apocalyptic anime, M.D. Geist is a cult classic of 80s OVA history.

## The Truth

The story begins on planet Jerra, where humanity has spread across multiple star systems. The Negsrom Army rebels are at war with the Jerra Regular Army. Geist was the commander of the Jerra special forces - a genetically engineered "Most Dangerous Soldier" so lethal in combat that he was deemed too dangerous to remain active. He was frozen in a stasis pod and launched into orbit.

Years later, the stasis satellite crashes back to Jerra, awakening Geist. He discovers a world devastated by war, where bandit gangs roam the ruins. After defeating a bandit's leader in combat, Geist takes control of the group. When he spots a Regular Army battleship under attack by Negsrom forces, he sees an opportunity to profit. He forms an uneasy alliance with Colonel Crutes and the Noah Guards crew to stop the Regular Army's doomsday device called "Death Force" - a system designed to annihilate all life on Jerra without discrimination.

Geist customizes a powered suit and leads an assault on the "Brain Palace" where Death Force's control computer is housed. During the invasion, all his comrades are killed. When he reaches the computer, Colonel Crutes reveals he intends to kill Geist as well, having activated a security robot. However, the robot fails to kill Geist, and Crutes is killed instead. After Crutes deactivates Death Force, Paia arrives and is horrified to learn Geist has reactivated it.

In the sequel M.D. Geist II: Death Force, most of Jerra's population has been decimated by Death Force. Geist continues dismantling the remaining Death Force machines. A warlord named Krauser - a precursor to Geist created through the same genetic engineering process - runs a refugee haven. Krauser lures Death Force machines into an artificial city and detonates a Jignix Bomb, but Geist's programming overrides his humanity and leads the machines away from the refugees, dooming them. Geist ultimately confronts and kills Krauser, and the final image shows his empty helmet.

## The Core

M.D. Geist represents a fascinating artifact of 1980s OVA culture - an era when the direct-to-video format allowed for darker, more mature content than television anime could typically accommodate. The film explores themes of genetic engineering and what it means to be human - Geist is essentially a weapon, his humanity stripped away through scientific modification. His lack of emotional response and pure combat efficiency raises questions about the cost of creating "perfect soldiers."

The post-apocalyptic setting prefigures later works in the genre, showing a world where warfare has become so extreme that total annihilation is considered an acceptable option. The film also touches on themes of isolation - Geist wakes up in a world he no longer understands, with no real connection to either side in the conflict.

Visually, M.D. Geist is notable for its detailed mechanical designs and its unflinching depiction of violence - unusual for its time. The film's influence can be seen in later cyberpunk works, and it remains a cult favorite among fans of obscure 80s anime. Director Koichi Ohata drew inspiration from Western films like Mad Max and The Terminator, creating a distinctly grittier anime that leans into the post-apocalyptic aesthetic before it became mainstream.
