# Sexmission (1984)

**Tags:** 🌍 Poland | 🎭 Satirical | 📚 Dystopia | 🧬 Rebellion

**Medium:** Film
**Raw Score:** 3.75/5
**Total Ratings:** 16000
**Creator/Author:** Juliusz Machulski (Director)

## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)

Sexmission (Seksmisja) is a 1984 Polish satirical science fiction comedy film directed by Juliusz Machulski. Set in the year 2044, the film follows two men, Max Paradys and Albert Starski, who volunteer for the first human hibernation experiment in 1991. Instead of waking up three years later as planned, they emerge into a post-nuclear world where males have become extinct and women now rule society through a totalitarian regime known as the League of Women.

The film is a cult classic in Poland, voted the best Polish film of the last 30 years in a 2005 poll of film magazine readers. It received the Złota Kaczka award for best Polish movie of 1984 and was fairly popular in Hungary when shown there. However, the film has been subject to feminist criticism due to its association of women's emancipation with communist dictatorship.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

In 1991, Maksymilian "Max" Paradys and Albert Starski volunteer themselves for the first human hibernation experiment, created by Professor Wiktor Kuppelweiser. Instead of being awakened three years later in 1994 as planned, they wake in the year 2044, in a post-nuclear world. They find themselves in a clinic being taken care of by women; Max becomes attracted to a woman named Lamia Reno.

After asking for Professor Kuppelweiser, they are informed that he "doesn't exist," and that there was a war long ago and that males have long been extinct. The men are under constant surveillance; Lamia informs them their society reproduces without males through parthenogenesis. During a briefing, Max kisses Lamia, for which she knocks him down and threatens both men with euthanasia. However, the kiss causes Lamia's drug-inhibited passions to resurface.

Max and Albert are permitted to go out to meet with Her Excellency, the supreme ruler of women. Waiting for her in the bio-sanctuary, they spot a tree with two tiny apples and eat them, having had enough of synthetic food. At the meeting they ask what womankind did to mankind. The women reply the extinction of men is not their fault, but Kuppelweiser's, who during the war invented an agent which was supposed to temporarily paralyze male genes, but instead destroyed male genes permanently.

Max proposes he and Albert serve as reproducers to restore the male population. However, the women do not wish the old order to return. Noticing the missing apples, Her Excellency becomes enraged and demands the men be confined again.

Max and Albert escape by damaging the electric power grid, but are ultimately caught. The women propose "naturalization" - undergoing sex reassignment surgery. When they refuse, a trial ensues where the women blame males for oppression, evil and vices, and praise their new society. They engage in historical revisionism by claiming that scientists such as Copernicus, Einstein, and Pincus were women. The assembly votes on whether the men should undergo forced 'naturalization' or be 'liquidated.' Naturalization is passed by one vote.

The men escape again and encounter other women who have never seen a man. They discover the nest of "decadence" - an anarchist women's group who do not wish to be part of the oppressive regime. During their escape, the men find a periscope revealing they live deep underground in expanded old mines. The periscope shows a dark, rocky landscape above ground with high radiation levels. However, it transpires that Lamia's "help" was a ruse to capture the men and force them into surgery.

In the hands of Tekla, the fate of the males is to have their organs extracted for transplantation and their remains used as food due to a protein shortage. The chief surgeon, Dr Yanda, is revealed to be Max's daughter, who now delights in taking revenge for his abandonment of his wife and child. Lamia sabotages the surgery and helps the men escape.

The men discover the "outside" is actually a painted canvas - a false barrier. When they cut through it, they find a beach and eventually a villa with food. The film's twist reveals that Her Excellency is actually a male in disguise, hidden since childhood when boys were "naturalized" into girls after the war. The government has been exaggerating radiation levels to keep inhabitants easier to control, and inhabitants are medicated to remove sexual desire.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)

**Totalitarianism and Control:** The film is a pointed satire on totalitarian systems, drawing parallels to the communist regime of Poland. The League of Women operates through manipulation, propaganda, and control - much like the communist government the film was created under. The exaggerated "radiation" keeping people underground mirrors state propaganda about the dangerous outside world. The secret meeting of Women's League apparatchiks and their lies to the women directly parallels the communist government of Poland.

**Gender and Power:** The film explores gender through an inverted lens - what happens when one gender holds total power over another? The film can be read as a critique of patriarchy by showing its mirror image, but also as a warning about any system that Concentrates power in one group. The "naturalization" process (forced sex reassignment) represents the ultimate erasure of identity.

**Propaganda and Reality:** The painted landscape representing the "radioactive outside" symbolizes how regimes construct false realities to maintain control. The heroes' discovery that the world outside is actually beautiful and habitable mirrors the fall of communism and the revelation that Western countries were not the hellscapes propaganda made them out to be.

**Consumerism and Conformity:** The League controls through both restriction and manufactured consent. The medication removing sexual desire represents pharmaceutical control, while the artificial apple tree represents manufactured scarcity. The film critiques how totalitarian systems maintain power through both prohibition and manipulation.

**Historical Revisionism:** The scene where women claim historical figures like Copernicus and Einstein were women is a satirical jab at how regimes rewrite history to serve their narrative. This extends to the broader theme of how those in power control the past to control the present.

Sexmission remains a fascinating artifact of Polish cinema - a film that used science fiction and comedy to deliver pointed political satire that resonated with audiences living under communism while remaining entertaining decades later.
