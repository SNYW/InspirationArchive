## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)

His Master's Voice is a 2018 Hungarian science fiction film directed by György Pálfi (Hukkle, Taxidermia). It is loosely based on the novel by Stanisław Lem.

The film follows a young man searching for his father, who disappeared while working on a classified project involving extraterrestrial signals from space.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

A young man believes he has seen his father in a documentary about a mysterious incident in Colorado. He embarks on a journey to find his father, who had fled Communist Hungary years earlier.

His search leads him to discover his father is now a wealthy university professor with a new family, but whose life is still affected by his previous work decoding a "message from space."

The film blends elements of mockumentary, using outdated cameras and formats, creating a paranoid, information-saturated atmosphere.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)

**Paranoia and Conspiracy:** The film explores government secrets, classified projects, and the search for truth.

**Father-Son Relationship:** The search for the father mirrors the search for meaning in a complex world.

**Lem's Legacy:** While loosely based on Lem's novel, the film captures his themes of communication, isolation, and the search for extraterrestrial intelligence.

**Visual Experimentation:** Pálfi's distinctive directing style creates a chaotic, multimedia-rich experience.
