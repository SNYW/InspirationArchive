## The Pitch

Biomega is a 2004-2009 cyberpunk manga by Tsutomu Nihei, the creator of Blame!. Set in a dystopian future on Earth and Mars, the story follows Zoichi Kanoe, a synthetic human created by TOA Heavy Industries, and his AI companion Fuyu—whose holographic consciousness is integrated into his motorcycle. They are sent to retrieve humans who have developed immunity to the N5S virus, a deadly infection from Mars that transforms humans into "Drones"—zombie-like creatures. As the infection spreads globally, Zoichi races against time to find survivors with the unique ability to resist and transmute the pathogen before the rival DRF organization captures them.

## The Truth

The story is set in the year 3006 AD, where the N5S virus has devastated humanity. The infection, originating from Mars, spreads rapidly across Earth, turning the majority of the population into Drones—grotesque, zombie-like beings devoid of higher consciousness.

TOA Heavy Industries ("East-Asia Industries") creates synthetic humans like Zoichi for the purpose of finding individuals who have adapted to resist the N5S virus. These "immune" humans are valuable to both TOA and their rivals, the Data Recovery Foundation (DRF), who seek to exploit their abilities.

Zoichi Kanoe is dispatched to a Maximum Security Containment Facility in south district 17 of 9JO to rescue Eon Green, a human with adaptive capabilities. However, the mission fails when Eon is captured by the Public Health Department. Undichi continues his search for her and other immeterred, Zounes.

Throughout the series, Zoichi encounters other synthetic humans, including Nishu Mizunoe, who is also searching for immune humans. The narrative explores the complex relationships between synthetic humans and their AI companions, as well as the political maneuvering between TOA Heavy Industries and the DRF.

A key subplot involves Go Hinoto, another synthetic human who discovers a new, less virulent pseudo-N5S virus. Before his death, he uploads his AI into orbit with this crucial information, which later becomes important to the story's resolution.

The manga's plot is known for its complex, non-linear storytelling, with multiple synthetic humans pursuing similar missions that gradually reveal the larger picture of the N5S pandemic and humanity's attempts to survive or recover from it.

## The Core

**Cyberpunk Foundations:** Biomega embodies core cyberpunk themes—megacorporations (TOA Heavy Industries, DRF), synthetic humans, AI companions, and a dystopian future where technology and humanity intertwine. Nihei continues his signature architectural aesthetics, featuring vast, detailed environments that emphasize humanity's smallness against massive industrial structures.

**Transhumanism and Identity:** The manga explores what it means to be human through synthetic humans like Zoichi, who possess artificial bodies but genuine consciousness and emotions. Their AI companions—Fuyu, Shin, Tyra—are not mere tools but entities with programmed emotions, raising questions about sentience, identity, and the boundaries between human and machine.

**Pandemic as Catalyst:** The N5S virus serves as both plot device and thematic exploration of how pandemics can reshape society, creating categories of the "infected" and "immune," and enabling power grabs by corporations and organizations that position themselves as humanity's saviors.

**Nihei's Visual Language:** Known for his atmospheric, detailed art, Nihei creates immersive, often claustrophobic environments—from futuristic cityscapes to sterile containment facilities. His work draws heavily from industrial and architectural aesthetics, with technology dominating every frame.

Biomega represents Nihei's evolution from Blame!, maintaining the cyberpunk setting and environmental storytelling while exploring different themes around humanity's survival and transformation in the face of existential threats.
