# Angel Cop (1989)

**Tags:** 🌍 Japan | 🎭 Gritty | 📚 Cyberpunk | 🧬 Rebellion, Religion, Megacorps, Transhumanism

**Medium:** OVA (Original Video Animation)

**Creator/Author:** Created and directed by Ichirō Itano; Written by Shō Aikawa and Ichirō Itano

## The Pitch

In a near-future Japan that dominates the global economy, a radical communist terrorist group called Red May threatens to destabilize the nation. The government responds by creating the Special Security Force—an elite unit granted immunity from the law to hunt down terrorists through any means necessary. But when the Special Security Force discovers that something far more dangerous than terrorists is hunting the radicals, they must confront psychic warriors called Hunters who believe they are divine instruments of justice, cleaning the world of the corrupt. This obscure 6-part OVA from the late 80s is a brutal cyberpunk actioner that explores themes of government overreach, moral absolutism, and the question of whether ends justify means.

## The Truth

The story unfolds in late 20th century Japan, where the country has become the world's largest economy. The communist radical group Red May wages a campaign of terrorism aimed at overthrowing the government and destabilizing the economic system. In response, the government establishes the Special Security Force (SSF)—a covert agency given complete autonomy and legal immunity to eliminate threats to national security.

The SSF is led by Chief Taki and includes several specialized agents: the protagonist Yo "Angel" Mikawa, a cold-hearted operative who believes in getting the job done regardless of casualties; her partner Isamu "Raiden" Sakada, who later undergoes cybernetic enhancement; Yuji "Hacker" Fuse, a powerful hulk who handles electronic warfare; and Reika "Peace," an explosives expert who secretly harbors feelings for Hacker.

Early missions involve hunting down Red May members, but the team soon discovers something is systematically eliminating the terrorists before they can be interrogated. The true threat emerges: three beings known as Hunters—psychic warriors who believe they have been divinely ordained to purge the world of corruption. These include Lucifer, a fanatical female hunter with telekinetic and telepathic powers who kills anyone she deems unjust, even those helping terrorists; Asura, a hunter with teleportation abilities who eventually allies with the SSF after Lucifer crosses moral lines; and Freya, a young pyrokinetic hunter.

The conspiracy deepens when it's revealed that Governor Maisaka of Tokyo, the highest police authority, orchestrated the entire situation. He created Red May as a false flag operation to justify establishing the SSF, then awakened the Hunters to eliminate both the terrorists and the SSF agents, covering up all evidence of his crimes.

In the final confrontation, Raiden sacrifices himself by detonating his cybernetic suit to destroy Lucifer. Angel kills Maisaka via a bomb planted by Chief Taki, while Taki himself is left mortally wounded but escapes. The surviving Hunters—Asura and Freya—depart, their purpose fulfilled. The ending leaves the future of Japan uncertain, with the system that created this tragedy apparently intact.

## The Core

Angel Cop is a fascinating artifact of late-80s OVA production, reflecting anxieties about corporate power, state violence, and moral absolutism that defined the era's cyberpunk genre. The series interrogates the concept of "extrajudicial action"—when does the state crossing legal and ethical lines become indistinguishable from the terrorism it claims to combat?

The Hunters represent a particularly interesting thematic element: they believe themselves to be instruments of divine justice, empowered to judge and execute anyone they deem corrupt. Yet their actions are ultimately exploitable by those in power, raising questions about moral absolutism and whether individuals should have the authority to decide who deserves death. Lucifer's logic is chillingly familiar—she sees herself as righteous, yet her victims include not just criminals but anyone who assists them, including children.

The series also examines cybernetic enhancement and what it means to be human. Raiden's transformation into a cyborg gives him power but isolates him from his humanity, ultimately requiring his sacrifice to destroy a threat too great for normal humans. The SSF agents themselves are essentially living weapons, disposable assets used by a corrupt system.

Visually, Angel Cop features the distinctive "Itano Circus"—a term for the anime style developed by Ichirō Itano featuring dramatic angle cuts, rapid zooming, and surreal imagery. The OVA is notorious for its over-the-top violence and includes some controversial content that led to censorship in English releases.

The series' cynical view of government and media anticipates themes that would become central to cyberpunk fiction, while its obscure status makes it a hidden gem for fans of the genre seeking alternatives to more famous works.
