# Captain Blood (1988)

**Tags:** 🌍 France | 🎭 Surreal | 📚 Science-Fantasy | 🧬 Sentience, Aliens, VR

**Medium:** Video Game
**Raw Score:** 4.1/5
**Total Ratings:** 15
**Creator/Author:** Exxos (ERE Informatique)

## The Pitch

Captain Blood is a groundbreaking 1988 French adventure game that tells the surreal story of Bob Morlock, a 1980s video game designer who picks "Captain Blood" as his nickname in tribute to the 1935 Errol Flynn film. While testing his own newly created space adventure game, Morlock becomes literally warped inside the spaceship of his own creation. Trapped in hyperspace, he accidentally gets cloned 30 times, each clone taking a portion of his vital fluid. For 800 years, the original Blood tracks down and disintegrates his clones—and when the game begins, he still has five remaining clones to destroy.

What makes Captain Blood remarkable is its innovative use of synthesized speech (especially on the Atari ST version), a soundtrack by legendary composer Jean-Michel Jarre, and its meta-narrative about a game designer trapped within his own creation. The game was a massive commercial success in Europe, selling over 100,000 copies and spawning sequels including Commander Blood (1994).

## The Truth

The player takes on the role of Captain Blood at a critical juncture: after 800 years of hunting clones across the galaxy, only five remain. These final clones have developed sentience and now operate independently across different planetary systems. Each clone controls a portion of the original Blood's vital essence, and destroying them is the only way for Blood to regain his full identity and finally escape the digital purgatory of his own game world.

The gameplay involves traveling between alien worlds, communicating with various extraterrestrial species using a unique icon-based interface, and hunting down each remaining clone. The game's innovative speech synthesis, particularly in the Atari ST version, created an atmospheric experience that was groundbreaking for its time. The title music is a stripped-down version of Jarre's "Ethnicolor," adding to the game's distinctive synth-driven ambiance.

Captain Blood represents a fascinating piece of gaming history from France's early adventure game era, developed by Exxos (formerly ERE Informatique) and published by Infogrames. The game's success led to multiple platform ports and sequels, establishing it as a cult classic of European gaming.

## The Core

Captain Blood stands as a pioneering example of meta-fiction in video games, predating similar concepts in mainstream gaming by decades. The narrative of a creator trapped within their own creation resonates with themes of authorship, identity, and the blurred boundaries between creator and creation. This self-referential premise—someone becoming trapped inside the very game they designed—anticipates later explorations of virtual reality and digital existence that would become major themes in science fiction.

The game's use of Jean-Michel Jarre's music connects it to France's distinctive electronic music tradition, while its innovative interface and speech synthesis demonstrated the creative potential of personal computers as artistic mediums. Captain Blood represents a significant moment in European game development, showing that the continent could produce original, ambitious content that rivaled American and Japanese productions.
