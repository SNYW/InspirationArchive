## The Pitch

First Squad: The Moment of Truth is a unique Russian-Japanese animated film that blends World War II historical drama with supernatural fantasy elements. Set during the opening days of WWII on the Eastern Front in 1941-1942, the film follows a group of Soviet teenagers with extraordinary abilities who are drafted into a special unit to fight the invading German army. Produced by Japan's renowned Studio 4°C and Russia's Molot Entertainment, the film stands out for its unusual East-Meets-West collaboration and its dark, gritty take on supernatural warfare.

The story centers on Nadya and her fellow teenage soldiers as they face not only the Nazi war machine but also an SS officer attempting to raise a supernatural army of crusader knights from the 12th century. Most of the teenage crew die in combat, leaving Nadya as the sole survivor who is taken to a secret Soviet laboratory studying supernatural phenomena. Her mission: dive into the world of the dead for reconnaissance, where she encounters her fallen comrades and must persuade them to continue fighting.

## The Truth

The film opens in the autumn and winter of 1941, as Nazi Germany launches Operation Barbarossa against the Soviet Union. The Soviet military has assembled a unit of teenagers with extraordinary psychic abilities—including telepathy, clairvoyance, and other supernatural gifts—to combat the German advance. These young soldiers are deployed against conventional German forces but quickly discover they face something far more terrifying: an SS officer known as the Crusader, who possesses the power to raise the dead.

The Crusader, a member of the Thule Society and follower of occult Nazi mysticism, seeks to resurrect an army of Teutonic Knights from the 12th Century—the Order of the Sacred Cross—who will fight as an invincible supernatural force for the Third Reich. Using ancient rituals and stolen artifacts, the Crusader begins raising these medieval warriors from their graves.

Nadya and her squad engage in fierce battles against both conventional German troops and the rising undead knights. Most of the teenagers are killed in combat, their bodies claimed by the supernatural warfare. Only Nadya survives the initial campaign. She is rescued by Soviet authorities and taken to a secret laboratory where scientists study paranormal phenomena, particularly communication with the dead.

The final act sees Nadya undertaking a dangerous psychic mission: she must dive into "the Gloomy Valley," a liminal space between the living and the dead, to gather intelligence on the Crusader's plans. There, she reunites with her fallen comrades and must convince their spirits to continue fighting alongside her. The film concludes with Nadya leading her dead comrades in a final assault against the Nazi supernatural forces.

## The Core

First Squad explores themes of sacrifice, loyalty, and the blurring boundaries between life and death. The film presents WWII not just as a political and military conflict but as a metaphysical battle between opposing forces of good and evil, with supernatural dimensions that extend beyond conventional historical narrative.

The collaboration between Japanese and Russian studios creates a distinctive visual and narrative style that combines Studio 4°C's bold, experimental animation techniques with Russian dark, atmospheric storytelling sensibilities. The film won the Kommersant newspaper's prize and the Kommerstandt Weekend Prize at the Moscow International Film Festival, as well as the Asteroide Award at the Trieste Science+Fiction Festival.

First Squad represents an unusual blend of historical war drama and supernatural fantasy—a genre combination that draws from both Japanese anime traditions and Russian literary sensibilities. Its focus on teenage soldiers and the psychological toll of supernatural warfare gives it a unique position in animated science fiction, distinguishing it from both conventional war films and typical anime narratives.
