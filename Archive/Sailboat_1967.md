# Sailboat (1967)

**Tags:** 🌍 USA | 🎭 Optimistic | 📚 Science-Fantasy | 🧬 Exploration, Time-Travel

**Medium:** Short Story
**Raw Score:** 3.5/5
**Total Ratings:** 25
**Creator/Author:** R.A. Lafferty

## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)

Sailboat is a whimsical, deeply optimistic tale from the golden age of science fiction that reads like a fever dream of pure joy. The story follows Jonathan Teague, an ordinary man who one day finds an extraordinary sailboat washed up on the shore of Lake Michigan—a boat that can sail not just through water, but through time and space itself. What unfolds is a picaresque adventure across centuries and worlds, as Teague discovers that the universe is far stranger and more magnificent than he ever imagined. Unlike the dark, cautionary tales that dominate SF, Sailboat is suffused with wonder, humor, and a profound sense that magic is real and waiting to be found. It's the kind of story that makes you remember why you first fell in love with science fiction.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

Jonathan Teague is an ordinary man leading an ordinary life in the Midwest—until the day a strange wooden sailboat washes up on the shore of Lake Michigan near his home. The boat is ancient-looking, made of wood and canvas, with no apparent means of propulsion beyond its single sail. Yet when Teague ventures out onto the lake, he discovers that this is no ordinary vessel: it can sail not just across water, but through time itself.

The boat responds to his thoughts and desires, carrying him backward and forward through time. He visits ancient civilizations, witnesses the founding of Rome, sees the fall of empires, and travels forward to futures both glorious and strange. But more importantly, he discovers that the boat is alive in some sense—it has a will and purpose of its own, and it seems to be guiding him toward some greater destiny.

Throughout his journey, Teague encounters a cast of colorful characters: time travelers like himself, beings from other dimensions, and the enigmatic builders of the sailboat itself. He learns that the boat is one of many such vessels, piloted by a loose brotherhood of adventurers who sail the currents of time. Some are seeking knowledge, others escape, others simply chasing wonder.

The story reaches its climax when Teague discovers the true nature of his sailboat: it is a tool for exploring the infinite possibilities of existence, a gift from a far-future humanity (or perhaps from beyond humanity entirely) to those rare souls with the courage to sail into the unknown. In the end, Teague must choose: return to his ordinary life, or continue sailing forever through the endless ocean of time.

He chooses to sail.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)

At its heart, Sailboat is a celebration of wonder and the human spirit's eternal longing for adventure. Unlike the paranoid, dystopian SF that dominated its era, Lafferty's story embraces the universe as a place of magic and possibility. The sailboat itself is a symbol of humanity's drive to explore, to push beyond boundaries, to see what lies over the next horizon.

The story also explores the nature of time not as a rigid framework but as an ocean—something to be navigated, explored, even worshipped. Time in Sailboat is not a river flowing in one direction but an infinite sea with currents and countercurrents, storms and calms, destinations waiting to be discovered.

What makes Sailboat particularly remarkable is its tone: playful, whimsical, almost childlike in its joy. Lafferty was always an outlier in SF, rejectng the genre's increasing focus on hard science and technological speculation in favor of folk tales, myths, and tall tales. Sailboat is quintessential Lafferty—odd, beautiful, and unlike anything else in the canon.

The story also carries a subtle philosophical message: that the ordinary world is just a thin veneer over something far greater. Teague's journey reveals that reality is far stranger and more magnificent than mundane existence suggests, and that anyone can sail beyond the familiar if they have the courage to climb aboard.
