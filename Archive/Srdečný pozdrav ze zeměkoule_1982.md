## The Pitch

Srdečný pozdrav ze zeměkoule (Best Regards from the Earth) is a 1982 Czechoslovak comedy science fiction film directed by Oldřich Lipský. The film follows two eccentric aliens from a distant galaxy who arrive on Earth in a flying saucer that conveniently takes the shape of a beer keg or waste container. The aliens land on Earth and set up residence at a waste dump, which they enthusiastically "colonize" before embarking on their mission to study average humans. Using the galaxy's central computer, they select Dr. Jánský, a shy scientist working at the Institute for Contact with Alien Civilizations, as their specimen of "typical humanity." The comedy ensues as the well-meaning but comically incompetent aliens misunderstand every aspect of human civilization, from everyday objects to social customs, offering a delightful fish-out-of-water satire on both alien and human perspectives.

## The Truth

The film opens with two aliens, affectionately nicknamed A and B, landing their spaceship—a shape-shifting saucer that can become a beer barrel or trash container—on a waste dump outside a Czechoslovak town. The aliens, who speak in deadpan earnestness despite their absurd misunderstandings, immediately begin their "research" by observing and interacting with human society.

Their chosen subject, Dr. Jánský, is a mild-mannered scientist at the Institute for Contact with Alien Civilizations who has spent his career waiting for contact that never came. The aliens essentially move in with him, much to his bewilderment, applying their alien logic to everything from human food to relationships. The comedy derives from the reversed perspective: humans appear as strange to the aliens as aliens do to humans.

Throughout the film, the aliens' misinterpretations of human behavior serve as a satirical mirror. When told about human customs, they apply their own galactic frameworks, turning mundane activities into philosophical dilemmas and ordinary objects into mysterious artifacts. Their earnest confusion about why humans do things "the way they do" highlights the arbitrary nature of cultural conventions.

The film was produced by the legendary Bratři v triku animation studio, known for their unique blend of live action and animation, and marks their only fully live-action feature film. This combination gives the film a distinctive visual style that complements its absurdist humor.

## The Core

The film operates on multiple levels of satire and commentary. Most obviously, it inverts the typical alien invasion narrative—instead of humanity being studied or conquered, two bumbling aliens become objects of bemused observation. This perspective shift allows director Oldřich Lipský to critique human society through an outsider's eyes, revealing the absurdity of everyday conventions that natives take for granted.

The choice of Czechoslovakia as both setting and country of origin is significant. Made during the late Communist era, the film's satire of bureaucratic institutions and "official" science (represented by the Institute with its official protocols for alien contact) carries an additional layer of pointed social commentary. Dr. Jánský represents the ordinary citizen waiting for permission from above, while the aliens represent a kind of innocent chaos that disrupts official order.

The film's comedy also functions as a commentary on communication and understanding. The aliens' fundamental failure to comprehend humans isn't due to malice or superior technology—it's simply that every system of understanding is culturally constructed. Their "mistakes" reveal how human society is equally arbitrary and constructed. The title's "best regards from Earth" becomes both a greeting and an ironic commentary on human self-perception.

Lipský, known for his work on animated classics like "The Fabulous World of Jules Verne," brings his characteristic whimsy and visual inventiveness to this live-action comedy, creating a uniquely Czechoslovak contribution to the science fiction comedy genre that remains charming and thoughtful decades after its creation.
