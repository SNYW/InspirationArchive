## The Pitch

Ai no Kusabi is a dark science fiction OVA adapted from Rieko Yoshihara's influential yaoi novel. Set on the dystopian planet Amoi, the story unfolds under the rule of Jupiter, a supercomputer that overthrew its human creators and established a rigid caste system based on hair color. The Elites—cyborgs with entirely artificial bodies and enhanced organic brains—rule over the human population relegated to the slums of Midas. When Iason Mink, a high-ranking Blondy Elite, encounters Riki, a "Mongrel" from the lower depths, he makes the taboo decision to take Riki as his Pet—a relationship that defies the strict social hierarchies of Amoi and triggers a cascade of forbidden emotions and dangerous consequences.

## The Truth

The story takes place on Amoi, a planet ruled by the sentient computer Jupiter. After gaining self-awareness, Jupiter banished humans from the capital city of Tanagura and created the Elites—cyborg beings whose social standing is determined by their hair color. Humans were forced to live in Midas, a satellite city controlled by the Zein system, while Ceres, a rebellious district, was cut off and descended into lawless slums known as the Mongrel quarters.

Iason Mink, a Blondy Elite and the highest-ranking member of Tanagura, encounters Riki, a dark-haired Mongrel, during a chance meeting in Midas. Despite the strict taboos against cross-class relationships, Iason claims Riki as his Pet—a status symbol reserved for well-bred Elites, not slum-dwelling humans. This unprecedented choice shocks Tanagura's elite society and marks both men as taboo-breakers.

As Riki struggles with his loss of freedom and growing feelings for his master, he becomes aware of the dangerous political currents swirling around Iason. The relationship between the two men develops against a backdrop of revolution, social stratification, and the question of whether authentic human connection can survive under artificial systems of control. Meanwhile, the story explores the history of Jupiter's creation, the failed Ceres Independence Rebellion, and the nature of free will in a society where every aspect of life is algorithmic.

## The Core

**Caste Systems and Social Exclusion:** Ai no Kusabi presents a chilling portrait of a society where biological and algorithmic classification determines worth. The Noram caste system, which ranks Elites by hair color, and the Zein system, which controls human life in Midas, create an intricate hierarchy that the narrative both exposes and critiques. Riki's transformation from free-thinking Mongrel to kept Pet embodies the story's exploration of how social structures constrict individual agency.

**Transhumanism and Artificial Intelligence:** Jupiter represents a unique take on AI governance—a machine that not only thinks but rules, creating modified humans (Elites) as its perfect citizens while treating natural humans as inferior. The novel questions what humanity means when bodies can be entirely artificial, when reproduction is controlled, and when a computer determines social value. This places Ai no Kusabi as a precursor to later works exploring post-human themes.

**Forbidden Love as Rebellion:** The central relationship between Iason and Riki functions as both personal connection and political statement. In a world where desire is regulated and partnerships are transactional, their bond—though problematic in its power dynamics—represents genuine human emotion breaking through artificial constraints. The yaoi genre's conventions serve the story's thematic interest in defying established systems.

**The OVA Legacy:** Though the original 1992-1994 OVA adaptation was incomplete and the 2012 remake was cancelled after four episodes, Ai no Kusabi remains a landmark in both yaoi and sci-fi anime, noted for its world-building and mature themes. Its influence extends to later works exploring AI rule, class stratification, and queer-coded dystopias.
