## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)

UFO: Afterlight is a 2007 real-time tactics and turn-based strategy video game developed by Altar Games, a Czech studio. It is the third installment in the UFO series, following UFO: Aftermath and UFO: Aftershock. Set on Mars, the game continues the story of human survival following the catastrophic alien invasion of Earth depicted in earlier titles. Players manage a colony of 10,000 humans in cryo-sleep, working to terraform the Red Planet while defending against emerging threats from ancient alien robotics and hostile extraterrestrial factions. The game received mixed reviews, holding a score of 71/100 on Metacritic.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

Following the canonical "bad" ending of UFO: Aftermath, Earth has fallen to the Reticulan alien invasion. Under the terms of the alien occupation, some humans were relocated to the floating city of Laputa above Earth (the events of UFO: Aftershock), while 10,000 colonists in cryo-sleep and a small research team were transported to Mars with the goal of terraforming the planet for human habitation.

The game begins when a member of the excavation research team, Jurgen, discovers an alien structure buried beneath the Martian surface filled with robots of unknown origin. When Jurgen activates the structure, the robots awaken and kill him—this serves as the tutorial mission. Shortly after, robots begin emerging from buried structures across Mars, attacking human stations and damaging critical infrastructure, including a water pipeline from the North Pole.

The colonists are forced to recover and manufacture armaments to defend against this new robotic threat. Once sufficiently armed, humans launch an assault on the robot cluster near the original discovery site. The attack succeeds, but a robot short-circuit activates a mysterious portal at the combat site. Aggressive alien creatures called "Beastmen" pour through the portal and open fire on the humans, forcing a retreat. The Beastmen refuse all attempts at communication and begin arriving in large numbers from multiple portals across the planet.

While humans eventually destroy the power source of the portals, the Beastmen establish numerous outposts, including a large base on the opposite side of Mars. They launch a direct attack on the human base—although repelled, the assault damages the cryo facility, initiating the process of awakening the colonists. The player faces a time limit to terraform and secure Mars before the awakened colonists overwhelm the small base's resources.

Humans must defend against both robots and Beastmen while researching advanced technologies for warfare and accelerated terraforming. Contact is restored with Earth, and humans reach the Cultists—the antagonist faction of UFO: Aftershock—exchanging weapon technologies. The Laputians then defeat and replace the Cultists as Earth's primary contact, sending materials, weapons, and scientific data to Mars after winning the war on Earth.

A different faction of Reticulans called the Expedition lands to the West and attacks both humans and the remaining Reticulans. The Expedition was sent from the Reticulan homeworld to hunt down the "rebel" Reticulans who occupied Earth. Players can align with either faction against the other, or fight both for their technology.

The final revelation pieces together Mars's history: the Beastmen were the planet's original inhabitants, while the "Martians" arrived from beyond the Solar System on a Myrmecol—a giant space-traveling psionic being of colossal size. The Beastmen were defeated and cast out through portals to a distant world. When the portals were reactivated by the human expedition, they returned to reclaim their homeworld.

The Martians can be allied after researching their language and helping them against the Beastmen. Ultimately, humans devise a virus targeting Beastmen DNA, requiring an Earth-like environment to spread—making terraforming essential. Once Mars is sufficiently terraformed, the virus is released, killing most Beastmen. The player leads the final assault on the Beastmen main base, achieving victory regardless of the status of other factions.

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)

**Human Survival and Adaptation:** The game explores humanity's resilience in the face of extinction. After losing Earth to alien invasion, humans must start over on a hostile world, managing limited resources and personnel across multiple demanding roles—combat, research, production, and base management. This reflects classic science fiction themes of humanity as a species willing to endure extreme hardship to survive.

**Terraforming as Transformation:** The necessity of terraforming Mars before the colonists awaken creates urgency that drives gameplay and narrative. This theme connects to broader SF questions about planetary transformation, human modification of environments, and what it means to make a world "human." The need to reshape Mars reflects humanity's drive to recreate Earth elsewhere—a theme common in hard SF.

**Alien Faction Complexity:** The game presents multiple alien factions with different agendas—the original Reticulans who occupied Earth, the Expedition sent to hunt them, the Martians, and the ancient hostile Beastmen. This complexity challenges simple human-vs-alien narratives, exploring themes of communication, negotiation, and the difficulty of distinguishing between hostile and potentially friendly extraterrestrial forces.

**Technology and Dependence:** Humans must research and adopt alien technologies to survive, creating a complex relationship with the very forces that destroyed Earth. The game questions whether using alien technology makes humans complicit in or similar to their oppressors—a theme of technological and moral compromise for survival.

**Resource Scarcity and Management:** The game emphasizes difficult choices about resource allocation, personnel deployment, and time management. With limited staff who can only be in one place at a time, players experience the pressure of survival on a hostile world where every decision has consequences. This reflects real-world resource management challenges and the difficult trade-offs required in extreme situations.

UFO: Afterlight represents Czech contributions to the science fiction strategy game genre, offering a mature, complex narrative about human survival against overwhelming odds on another world. While not as well-known as its predecessors or competitors, it provides an engaging examination of themes central to hard science fiction: exploration, adaptation, and the eternal human drive to survive and expand beyond Earth.
