# Nexus (1981)

**Medium:** Graphic Novel

**Creator/Author:** Mike Baron (Writer) & Steve Rude (Artist)

## The Pitch

Five hundred years in the future, on the moon city of Ylum (a shortened form of "asylum"), a reluctant hero named Horatio Valdemar Hellpop receives his powers from an alien entity called the Merk. But there's a catch: as payment for his abilities, Nexus must hunt down and execute a certain number of mass murderers each cycle—humanity's worst killers. When the Merk selects a target, Nexus suffers agonizing headaches and violent dreams of the victims until he carries out the execution. It's a dark bargain: a superhero bound by contract to kill, questioning his role while defending a lunar refuge that serves as sanctuary for the universe's outcasts.

## The Truth

The series follows Nexus (real name Horatio Valdemar Hellpop), who serves as the protector of Ylum, a utopian lunar city that functions as a refuge for telepaths, mutants, and others persecuted across the solar system. Nexus possesses immense powers: superhuman strength, flight, energy projection, and telepathy. However, these powers come with an obligation to the Merk—an enigmatic alien entity that grants his abilities in exchange for his service as an executioner.

Each time the Merk designates a mass murderer as a target, Nexus receives detailed information about the target's crimes through agonizing psychic visions. These visions are so intense that they cause physical injuries to Nexus's body, mimicking the violence inflicted by the murderer on their victims. He has no choice but to track down and kill the target to end his suffering and fulfill his contract.

Throughout the series, Nexus struggles with the moral implications of his role. He questions whether he's a hero protecting the innocent or merely a weapon wielded by an alien master. The citizens of Ylum worship him as a savior, but he sees himself as a reluctant assassin. The city itself is a fascinating blend of high technology and social idealism—a place built on tolerance and refuge, yet defended by someone whose hands are perpetually stained with blood.

The comics explore Nexus's relationships with the various factions of Ylum, his encounters with other solar system powers, and his ongoing psychological battle with the Merk's influence. The series is notable for its complex protagonist who never fully comes to terms with his role, and for its thoughtful examination of vigilante justice, moral responsibility, and the cost of power.

## The Core

At its heart, Nexus is a meditation on the ethics of violence and the nature of heroism. Unlike traditional superheroes who fight for abstract ideals of justice, Nexus is bound by a literal contract—he kills not out of moral conviction but out of obligation. This creates a fascinating tension between his desire to protect Ylum's utopian ideals and his role as the Merk's executioner.

The series also explores themes of refuge and exile. Ylum represents the ultimate sanctuary—a place where telepaths, genetic anomalies, and society's outcasts can live in peace. But this peace is maintained by a warrior who brings violence from the outside world into their haven. It's a profound commentary on how even utopian societies depend on violence and coercion to maintain their existence.

Baron's writing is sharply satirical, using the futuristic setting to comment on contemporary issues like media saturation, corporate power, and the manipulation of history. The series is set in a universe where a computerized library controls all historical memory, where merchants overwhelm telepathic channels with advertising, and where the remnants of old empires (like the Soviet Empire) still pose threats. This combination of superhero action with genuine social commentary makes Nexus a groundbreaking work that transcends its genre roots.
