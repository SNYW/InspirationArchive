# The Technopriests (1998)

**Medium:** Graphic Novel

**Creator/Author:** Alejandro Jodorowsky (Writer), Zoran Janjetov (Artist)

**Tags:** 🌍 France | 🎭 Cerebral | 📚 Science-Fantasy | 🧬 Transhumanism, Religion, Evolution

## The Pitch

In a distant future where technology and spirituality have merged into an all-consuming religion, three brothers are born from a priestess violated by space pirates. Albino, the youngest, rejects his mother's cheese-making empire to pursue his dream of becoming a video game creator—but fate propels him instead into the ranks of the Technopriests, a cosmic order of tech-practicing monks. As he rises through the ranks toward becoming the Supreme Technopriest, Albino must navigate a universe where scientific advancement threatens to eclipse human connection, all while dictating his memoirs aboard a spaceship carrying half a million young acolytes to a promised galaxy.

## The Truth

The Technopriests unfolds across three interconnected narrative threads: Albino's present-day journey as the Supreme Technopriest leading 500,000 young technopriests to the promised galaxy; his origin story and rise to power; and the parallel experiences of his family during his ascent.

The story begins when pirates attack the sacred asteroid where Panepha, a virgin oracle at the Imperial Palace, resides. They rape her, and she gives birth to three children: Almagro, Albino, and Onyx. Panepha rejects Onyx, who goes on to create and lead the Great Kamenvert Factory. Albino, unhappy with the family cheese-making business, convinces his mother to send him to Don Mossimo's technopriest training school.

At the school, Albino discovers he has exceptional abilities. He rises through the ranks, facing numerous trials and obstacles. His older brother Almagro becomes the most favored child of Panepha and a powerful figure, while Albino struggles against the institutional corruption within the Technopriest hierarchy.

The series follows Albino's transformation from a reluctant student to the Supreme Technopriest—a leader tasked with guiding his people to a new galaxy. Throughout his journey, he maintains his belief that human relationships and emotions must be valued more highly than pure scientific advancement. His companion Tinigrifi serves as co-narrator and emotional anchor throughout their journey.

The narrative explores the tension between technological perfection and human warmth, as the Technopriests represent a future where science has become indistinguishable from religion, and where the line between creator and creation has blurred beyond recognition.

## The Core

The Technopriests represents Jodorowsky's meditation on the deification of technology and the spiritual emptiness that accompanies unchecked scientific progress. The Technopriests themselves function as a religious order where technology has replaced theology—they are monks of the machine age, worshipping at the altar of innovation while losing touch with their own humanity.

The central theme explores whether humanity can evolve technologically without losing its soul. Albino's persistent belief that "human relationships will be valued more highly than scientific advances" serves as the counterpoint to the Technopriests' increasingly cold technological worship. This tension mirrors contemporary debates about artificial intelligence, transhumanism, and whether our technological children will retain any meaningful connection to their human creators.

The graphic novel also functions as a critique of institutional religion and power structures. The Technopriest hierarchy is corrupt, hierarchical, and more concerned with control than enlightenment—mirroring Jodorowsky's skepticism toward organized spirituality of all kinds. Albino's journey is thus not just a coming-of-age story but a philosophical quest to find a middle path between technological coldness and spiritual stagnation.

Visually, Janjetov's artwork blends science fiction imagery with gothic and psychedelic elements, creating a universe that feels both alien and strangely familiar—a cosmic cathedral of rust and light where ancient religious iconography mingles with futuristic technology.
