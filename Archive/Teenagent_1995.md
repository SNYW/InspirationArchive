## The Pitch (Non-Spoiler Breakdown)

Teenagent is a 1995 Polish point-and-click adventure game developed by Metropolis Software House, notable as the first CD-ROM game released in Poland. The player controls Mark Hopper, a teenage boy who dreams of becoming a secret agent. When gold begins disappearing across Poland, the police agency decides to hire an ordinary citizen to investigate—choosing the unlikely hero Mark for the job. The game combines comedy, adventure, and spy themes in a style inspired by the 1991 film "If Looks Could Kill." Originally released for MS-DOS and Amiga platforms, Teenagent achieved international recognition and was later released on GOG.com as their 100th game.

## The Truth (Full Story Breakdown)

The game is divided into three main sections that Mark must complete:

**"The Three Tasks"** serves as the opening chapter where Mark must prove himself worthy of becoming an agent. He must complete three tasks assigned by the captain of the guards to demonstrate his worthiness. These tasks test his problem-solving abilities and establish the comedic tone of the adventure.

**"The Village"** brings Mark to a rural village where he must investigate the gold disappearances. He must wander around the village, interact with various characters, and gather information. The goal is to gain entry to a mansion owned by the mysterious villain behind the gold disappearances. This section involves typical adventure game puzzles—finding items, talking to NPCs, and solving environmental challenges.

**"The Mansion"** is the final section where Mark must infiltrate the villain's lair and put a stop to the plans causing gold to vanish across Poland. This climactic chapter requires the player to use everything learned in previous sections to outwit the antagonist and complete Mark's transformation from aspiring agent to real hero.

The character of Mark Hopper was voiced by Adrian Chmielarz's younger brother, who would later become a prominent game developer himself. The game's soundtrack was composed by Polish artist Radek Szamrej (RST).

## The Core (Themes & Meaning)

**Polish Game Development History:** Teenagent represents a milestone in Polish video game history as one of the earliest Polish games to pursue international release. Developed by Metropolis Software House, it helped establish Poland's presence in the adventure game genre during a time when Polish game development was still in its infancy. The game's success paved the way for subsequent Polish titles and demonstrated that Polish developers could compete on the international stage.

**Coming-of-Age Spy Comedy:** The game blends the coming-of-age narrative with spy thriller elements, creating a unique tone that differs from typical adventure games. Mark's journey from ordinary teenager to secret agent reflects youthful fantasies of heroism while maintaining a lighthearted, comedic approach. The inspiration from "If Looks Could Kill" is evident in the film's influence on the game's premise of an ordinary teen getting caught up in espionage.

**First Polish CD-ROM Release:** As the first game released on CD-ROM in Poland, Teenagent marked a technological milestone in Polish gaming. This format allowed for expanded content, including voice acting and richer audio—features that distinguished it from earlier disk-based releases.
