## The Pitch

Wicked City is a 1987 Japanese adult animated neo-noir action film that explores the hidden boundary between the human world and the "Black World" — an alternate dimension populated by supernatural demons. The story follows Renzaburō Taki, a Black Guard agent who protects the treaty between humans and demons, partnered with Makie, a beautiful demon woman. Together, they must protect a 200-year-old mystic who signs a crucial peace treaty, while battling radical demons who seek to destroy the fragile harmony between worlds. Directed by Yoshiaki Kawajiri (who would later direct Ninja Scroll), this is a stylish, violent, and sexually charged supernatural action film that predates the cyberpunk boom.

## The Truth

The story takes place in late 20th century Tokyo, where the secret world of demons has coexisted with humanity for centuries. A peace treaty between the two worlds has been maintained by the Black Guard — a secret organization of agents from both worlds.

Renzaburō Taki lives a double life: by day he's a salaryman for an electronics company, by night he's a Black Guard agent. After a mysterious woman named Kanako — revealed to be a spider-like demon — attacks him and steals a sample of his genetic material, Taki is assigned to protect Giuseppe Mayart, a 200-year-old mystic who serves as a signatory for the new peace treaty between humans and the Black World.

Taki is partnered with Makie, a Black Guard member from the Black World who also works as a fashion model. The trio takes shelter in a hotel with spiritual barriers, but Mayart sneaks out to a soapland (adult bathhouse), where he is captured by radicals who sap his health. Makie is later kidnapped by a tentacle demon, and Taki pursues the radical leader Mr. Shadow to rescue her.

During the rescue, Taki discovers Makie being violated by radicals. He kills her attackers but is subsequently fired from the Black Guard for his recklessness. The pair later make love in a church, and Makie reveals her past involvement with radicals and her reasons for believing in peace.

In the final confrontation, Mr. Shadow attacks again, but Mayart reveals his true purpose: he was hired to protect Taki and Makie, who were selected as the first couple capable of producing half-human, half-demon children — essential for the new peace treaty. Makie's powers increase due to her pregnancy, and she delivers the final blow to Shadow. Though angry at being manipulated, Taki accepts his role to protect both worlds and his family.

## The Core

Wicked City is a fascinating blend of noir aesthetics, supernatural horror, and political allegory. The film explores the theme of coexistence between different species — a metaphor for multiculturalism and the difficulty of maintaining peace between fundamentally different groups. The Black Guard represents the diplomatic and military apparatus that maintains uneasy truces, while the radicals embody the violent opposition to any form of integration.

The film also touches on questions of identity and belonging. Taki is torn between his human life and his duties as a Black Guard, while Makie has abandoned her radical past to fight for peace. Their relationship — chosen for them by the authorities specifically for genetic compatibility — raises questions about whether love can be engineered for political purposes.

Kawajiri's direction is notable for its kinetic fight scenes, gothic atmosphere, and mature themes. The film was controversial for its sexual violence, but defenders argue it uses such elements purposefully within its dark fantasy framework. The film predates the cyberpunk boom but shares similar aesthetics, featuring a neon-lit Tokyo and themes of hidden worlds beneath the surface of everyday reality.

The film's treatment of female power is complex: Makie is simultaneously a sex object and an incredibly powerful warrior, with her abilities growing alongside her pregnancy. Critics have noted this reflects both feminist power fantasy and problematic male gaze simultaneously.
