# Woman in the Moon (1929)

## The Pitch

Woman in the Moon (Frau im Mond) is a groundbreaking German silent science fiction film directed by Fritz Lang, considered one of the first "serious" science fiction films ever made. Released in 1929, it presented the basics of rocket travel to a mass audience for the first time, including the revolutionary use of a multi-stage rocket design. The film tells the story of an entrepreneur named Helius who finances a rocket expedition to the Moon after learning from Professor Mannfeldt that the Moon may contain vast deposits of gold. Alongside a crew including his assistant Friede, the professor, and other team members, Helius embarks on what becomes a perilous journey to the Moon's far side, where they discover a breathable atmosphere and confirm the presence of gold—but also face a deadly crisis when their oxygen supplies run low and one person must make the ultimate sacrifice.

## The Truth

The film begins with Helius, an entrepreneur with dreams of space travel, seeking out Professor Mannfeldt, a visionary who has written a treatise claiming the Moon contains abundant gold. A gang of criminal businessmen also becomes interested in Mannfeldt's theories and attempts to force Helius to include them in his expedition. Eventually, Helius assembles his rocket team: himself, Professor Mannfeldt and his pet mouse Josephine, his assistant Windegger, Friede (whom Helius secretly loves), and Turner (the spy).

During the journey, they discover a young stowaway named Gustav. As they approach the Moon's far side, they find it has a breathable atmosphere—contrary to scientific belief at the time. Mannfeldt discovers gold, validating his theories, but dies during a confrontation with Turner. Turner himself is killed in a struggle, and gunfire damages the oxygen tanks, leaving the crew with insufficient oxygen for all to return to Earth.

The crew faces an impossible choice: someone must remain on the Moon. They draw straws, and Windegger loses. However, Helius decides to drug Windegger and take his place, allowing Windegger to return to Earth with Friede. But Friede discovers the plan, pretends to drink the drugged wine, and locks herself in her compartment. Helius makes Gustav his co-pilot, and the rocket departs with Friede choosing to stay behind with Helius on the Moon rather than leave him alone. The film ends with them embracing on the lunar surface, preparing to survive together on the Moon.

## The Core

**Early Rocket Science Fiction:** Woman in the Moon was revolutionary in its attempt to present realistic rocket science to a mass audience. Lang and his team consulted with rocket scientists and engineers to create what was then the most technically accurate depiction of space travel on film. The multi-stage rocket design shown predated actual multi-stage rockets by decades, and the film's attention to weightlessness, rocket propulsion, and the challenges of space travel set a new standard for science fiction cinema.

**Romantic Sacrifice and Gender Dynamics:** The film explores themes of romantic love and sacrifice, with a complex emotional triangle between Helius, Friede, and Windegger. Notably, Friede demonstrates agency and intelligence throughout—she is the one who discovers Helius's plan to sacrifice himself and chooses to stay with him. The film also features one of the first positive depictions of a woman in a scientific/space exploration context.

**Economic Critique:** Beneath the adventure narrative lies a critique of capitalism and the pursuit of wealth. The expedition is motivated by gold, and the film questions whether such economic incentives are worthy motivations for exploration. The criminal gang's involvement represents the corrupting influence of greed on scientific endeavor.

**Technical Innovation:** The film's special effects were groundbreaking for their time, and it features the first depiction of a countdown to rocket launch in cinema—a now-standard trope. The production design and technical accuracy influenced generations of science fiction filmmakers that followed.
