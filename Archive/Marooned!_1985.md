## The Pitch

Marooned! is a text-graphical adventure game released in 1985 for the TRS-80 Color Computer (CoCo), an 8-bit home computer manufactured by Radio Shack. Developed by Steve Hartford and published by Saguaro Software, the game places players in the role of an ordinary person who witnesses a UFO landing in their corn field. What begins as curiosity quickly transforms into a fight for survival as the player is abducted by extraterrestrials and left on a mysterious alien world. The player must explore the alien environment, collect items, solve puzzles, and find a way to escape back to Earth.

## The Truth

The game opens with the player sitting on their back porch when they witness a strange, flashing UFO descend from the clouds and land in a nearby corn field. Driven by curiosity, the player approaches the craft and enters it—only for the UFO to immediately take off, transporting the player to an alien world.

Once stranded, the player must navigate through various locations on the alien planet, collecting items and solving inventory-based puzzles to progress. The game features a simple parser that accepts basic commands, allowing players to move between rooms, examine objects, pick up items, and use them in combination with environmental elements. Unlike many adventure games of the era, Marooned! includes visual graphics alongside text descriptions, though the illustrations are rudimentary by modern standards.

The gameplay involves exploring a series of connected areas on the alien world, managing inventory, and finding the correct sequence of actions to ultimately repair or activate a means of escape. The game includes a save system with nine slots, allowing players to record their progress. The graphics, while simple, include small animated elements—most notably the flashing lights of the UFO that continue to blink even when the player accesses other screens.

## The Core

Marooned! represents a slice of early 1980s American home computer gaming—a period when independent developers could create and distribute games for platforms like the TRS-80 Color Computer without the oversight of major publishers. The game embodies the adventure game genre's fascination with alien encounter narratives, combining elements of classic sci-fi stories about ordinary people thrust into extraordinary circumstances.

The game's limited parser and minimal interface reflect the technical constraints and design philosophies of early 1980s adventure games. The focus on inventory puzzles and exploration over combat or complex narrative mirrors the broader adventure game tradition of the era. As a piece of microcomputing history, Marooned! illustrates the grassroots nature of game development during the home computer revolution, when individual programmers like Steve Hartford could create and publish games independently.

The title fits within a tradition of "marooned" survival narratives in science fiction—from classic novels like Robinson Crusoe to modern adaptations like "The Martian." The interactive nature of gaming adds a layer of agency to these themes, as players must actively work to survive and return home rather than passively observe a character's journey. The game's straightforward premise—witness an alien landing, get abducted, must escape—distills these themes into a compact interactive experience representative of early sci-fi adventure gaming.
