## The Pitch

Lily C.A.T. is a 1987 cosmic horror anime OVA that pays heavy homage to Ridley Scott's Alien and John Carpenter's The Thing. Set in the 23rd century, the story follows the deep-space cruiser Saldes, hired by the Syncam Corporation to shuttle surveyors to investigate a relatively new planet for mining rights. The crew and passengers enter cryogenic hypersleep for the 20-year journey, aging only one month biologically.

When the ship awakens, the crew discovers that two of the surveyors are impostors—and far worse, an extraterrestrial bacterium has been released from collected space debris. This alien pathogen proves capable of mimicking human forms and evolves rapidly, killing crew members by dissolving their bodies while leaving clothes intact. As the infection spreads and the ship's computer is overrun, the survivors must confront not just the alien threat but also the truth about their own identities and the corporation that sent them.

## The Truth

The voyage begins normally enough: the surveyors and crew enter hypersleep, but during transit the ship's computer detects debris and collects a sample. Upon awakening, the crew learns that two surveyors are impostors—but this revelation is quickly overshadowed by the discovery of Morgan W. Scott, found dead from a mysterious infection that dissolves his body while leaving his clothes pristine. Dr. Mead, mechanic Guy, and Watts soon succumb to the same fate.

The bacteria then evolves into a shapeshifting life-form capable of impersonating its victims, killing Farrah Van Dorothy in the sickbay. Meanwhile, the impostors are revealed: Jiro Takagi is actually a medical student who murdered three drug dealers responsible for his sister's overdose, while Dick Berry is a detective tracking him. The decades spent in cryo-sleep mean Jiro's crimes are technically beyond prosecution, but Berry remains fixated on bringing him in.

As the death toll mounts, Captain Hamilton reveals a tragic backstory—he is 240 years old, having spent centuries in deep space missions while everyone he ever knew aged and died. The only creature that can navigate the ship is Nancy's cat, Lily... or rather, a robotic replica called "The Master" or Lily-C.A.T. (Computerized Animal-shaped Technological robot).

In the climactic confrontation, Berry dies from the infection. Jiro attempts suicide until Hamilton reveals a shuttle as their only escape. The ending remains ambiguous about whether any of them survive—the final frames show the ship drifting, the bacterial entity possibly having taken over.

## The Core

**Alien Imitation and Identity Crisis:** The film explores themes of identity through the alien pathogen's ability to perfectly mimic human forms. This creates paranoia among the survivors—who is real, who is infected? The impostor subplot reinforces this by revealing that people are not who they claim to be even before the alien threat emerges.

**Isolation and Temporal Displacement:** Captain Hamilton's tragic backstory embodies extreme isolation—the only escape from losing everyone he loves is to keep running into space. The 20-year cryogenic jumps mean decades pass for the universe while the crew ages barely at all, creating a unique form of temporal alienation.

**Corporate Indifference:** The reveal that Syncam Corporation wanted to study the bacteria regardless of human cost adds a layer of corporate critique. The surveyors were essentially expendable test subjects for the company's scientific objectives.

**Homage as Identity:** The film is often dismissed as derivative, but its strength lies in synthesizing elements from Alien (corporate ship, chestburster equivalent, final girl/hero) and The Thing (body horror, shape-shifting, paranoia, para-scientific explanation). It's a genre exercise that rewards fans of cosmic horror.

**Technical Excellence:** The animation by Studio Pierrot, character designs by Yasuomi Umetsu, and monster designs by Yoshitaka Amano create a visually distinctive work that stands out among 1980s OVAs. The 67-minute runtime delivers concentrated horror atmosphere.
