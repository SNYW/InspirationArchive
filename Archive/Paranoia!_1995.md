## The Pitch

Paranoia! is a Czech real-time strategy game released in 1995, developed by Phoenix Arts and recognized as the first Czech RTS ever created. Designed as a clone of the influential Dune II, the game is set in the year 2673 in the Beretta solar system on the planet Paranoia, where a brutal war has raged for four years over a scarce resource—a mysterious plant called the hopsinka with special powers. The player commands the Terrans, the native population, in their struggle to reclaim territories occupied by alien forces. Originally conceived as a freeware project, rising costs led developers to release it commercially in September 1995 after approximately one year of development. A sequel, Paranoia II, followed in 1996.

## The Truth

The game's narrative centers on the Aponid War, a conflict that began when the barren planet Paranoia's most valuable resource—a plant called the hopsinka—was discovered to possess special powers. Three rival factions vie for control of this strategic resource, though the player takes command of the Terrans, the planet's native human population.

The gameplay follows the Dune II formula: players must gather resources, construct bases, train units, and engage in real-time combat against enemy forces. The science fiction setting provides various unit types and structures typical of the genre, though the game did not receive significant mainstream attention outside of Czech gaming circles.

Paranoia! was notable primarily for being the first real-time strategy game developed in Czechoslovakia (by then the Czech Republic), representing an early milestone in Czech video game development. Developer Radek Ševčík created the project specifically because he wanted to make a better clone of Dune II, addressing aspects of that game he disliked. The development team at Phoenix Arts expanded on his original vision, and the project evolved from its initial freeware conception into a commercial release.

The game has achieved a minor cult status in recent years, with developer Spytihněv releasing "Shrot" in 2024—a tribute game that mimics Paranoia!'s retro 2D visual style while updating the gameplay.

## The Core

Paranoia! represents an important artifact in the history of Czech gaming, being the nation's first real-time strategy title. While technically a Dune II clone, it demonstrates the global influence of the RTS genre and how developers worldwide adapted the formula established by Westwood's landmark game.

The game's setting in a distant future (2673) with resource scarcity driving conflict reflects common science fiction themes of territorial dispute and survival. The hopsinka—a plant with special powers serving as the catalyst for war—embodies the trope of valuable resources sparking interstellar conflict, a theme prevalent in space opera narratives.

From a historical perspective, Paranoia! documents an early attempt at game development in post-communist Czechoslovakia/Czech Republic, showing how local developers adapted Western gaming trends to create domestically produced titles. The existence of a 2024 tribute game (Shrot) indicates that Paranoia! maintains a nostalgic place in Czech gaming culture, with newer developers acknowledging its historical significance.

The game's relative obscurity—it was never widely distributed outside of Central Europe—makes it a genuine artifact of regional game development history rather than a well-known international title.
